# Thin Lens Equations

## Introduction

Thin lens equations form the cornerstone of optical calculations in clinical optometry. These mathematical relationships allow practitioners to determine image location, size, and characteristics for any combination of object position and lens power.

---

## Part 1: The Thin Lens Assumption

### 1.1 Definition of a Thin Lens

A thin lens is defined as a lens whose thickness is negligible compared to its focal length and object/image distances. This assumption simplifies calculations by:

1. Ignoring the distance between front and back surfaces
2. Assuming the lens has no thickness for ray-tracing purposes
3. Measuring all distances from the optical center

---

## Part 2: The Lensmaker's Equation

### 2.1 Formula

The Lensmaker's equation calculates the power of a thin lens from its physical properties:

**P = (n - 1) [(1/r₁) - (1/r₂)]**

Where:
- P = lens power in diopters (D)
- n = refractive index of lens material
- r₁ = radius of curvature of front surface (meters)
- r₂ = radius of curvature of back surface (meters)

### 2.2 Sign Convention

**Front Surface (r₁):**
- Positive (+): Surface convex toward incident light
- Negative (-): Surface concave toward incident light

**Back Surface (r₂):**
- Positive (+): Surface convex toward incident light
- Negative (-): Surface concave toward incident light

---

## Part 3: The Thin Lens Equation

### 3.1 Fundamental Formula

**1/f = 1/do + 1/di**

Or in terms of power:

**P = 1/do + 1/di**

### 3.2 Sign Convention

| Quantity | Positive (+) | Negative (-) |
|----------|--------------|--------------|
| Object distance (do) | Real object (standard) | Rarely used |
| Image distance (di) | Real image (opposite side) | Virtual image (same side) |
| Focal length (f) | Converging lens | Diverging lens |

---

## Part 4: Magnification

### 4.1 Linear Magnification Formula

**M = -di / do = h' / h**

### 4.2 Interpretation

| M Value | Image Character |
|---------|----------------|
| |M| > 1 | Magnified |
| |M| < 1 | Minified |
| |M| = 1 | Same size |
| M > 0 | Upright |
| M < 0 | Inverted |

---

## Part 5: Clinical Applications

### 5.1 Correcting Refractive Errors

**Myopia:** Correct with minus lens that images infinity at the far point
**Hyperopia:** Correct with plus lens that images infinity at the virtual far point

### 5.2 Near Vision

**Presbyopia:** Add plus power for near work
Total near power = distance power + add

---

## Summary

**Key Formulas:**
- Lensmaker's Equation: P = (n-1)[(1/r₁) - (1/r₂)]
- Thin Lens Equation: 1/f = 1/do + 1/di
- Linear Magnification: M = -di/do
