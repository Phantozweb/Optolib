# Tear Osmolarity Testing

## Introduction

Tear osmolarity measurement is considered one of the most sensitive and specific tests for diagnosing and monitoring dry eye disease. Elevated osmolarity is a core mechanism in the pathophysiology of dry eye.

## Clinical Significance

**Role in Dry Eye:**
- Elevated osmolarity (>308 mOsm/L) is diagnostic
- Indicates hyperosmolar stress on ocular surface
- Correlates with disease severity
- Useful for monitoring treatment response

## Measurement Methods

### TearLab Osmolarity System

**Principle:**
- Impedance-based measurement
- Requires 50 nL tear sample
- Results in approximately 30 seconds

**Procedure:**
1. Calibrate device
2. Touch sensor to inferior tear meniscus
3. Hold until sample collected
4. Read result from display

### Normal and Abnormal Values

| Osmolarity (mOsm/L) | Interpretation |
|---------------------|----------------|
| <308 | Normal |
| 308-316 | Borderline |
| >316 | Abnormal (dry eye) |
| >8 mOsm/L inter-eye difference | Diagnostic for dry eye |

## Clinical Utility

**Advantages:**
- Objective measurement
- High sensitivity and specificity
- Quick and easy to perform
- Useful for monitoring treatment

**Limitations:**
- Variability between measurements
- Cost of equipment and consumables
- Affected by recent eye drop use
- Temperature sensitivity

## Interpretation Guidelines

1. **Single Eye Measurement:**
   - >308 mOsm/L suggests dry eye
   - Higher values indicate more severe disease

2. **Inter-eye Difference:**
   - >8 mOsm/L difference is significant
   - Indicates asymmetric tear film dysfunction

3. **Serial Measurements:**
   - Useful for monitoring treatment response
   - Decreasing values suggest improvement

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Normal tear osmolarity is <308 mOsm/L
2. >316 mOsm/L is diagnostic for dry eye
3. Inter-eye difference >8 mOsm/L is significant
4. Most sensitive single diagnostic test
5. Useful for monitoring treatment response
6. Wait after eye drop use before testing
