# Inflammatory Mechanisms in Dry Eye

## Introduction

Inflammation is both a cause and consequence of dry eye disease. Understanding the inflammatory cascade is essential for selecting appropriate anti-inflammatory treatments.

## Key Inflammatory Mediators

### Cytokines

**Pro-inflammatory Cytokines:**
- IL-1α and IL-1β
- IL-6
- IL-8
- TNF-α
- IL-17

**Role in Dry Eye:**
- Upregulated in dry eye
- Recruit inflammatory cells
- Promote tissue damage
- Detected by MMP-9 testing

### Matrix Metalloproteinases

**MMP-9:**
- Key marker of inflammation
- Degrades extracellular matrix
- Disrupts tight junctions
- Detected by InflammaDry test

**Other MMPs:**
- MMP-1, MMP-2, MMP-3
- Contribute to tissue remodeling
- Elevated in dry eye

## T-Cell Involvement

### CD4+ T Cells

**Subtypes in Dry Eye:**
- Th1 cells: produce IFN-γ
- Th17 cells: produce IL-17
- Contribute to chronic inflammation

**Mechanism:**
1. Dry eye triggers inflammation
2. Antigen presentation activates T cells
3. T cells infiltrate lacrimal gland and conjunctiva
4. Cytokine release perpetuates inflammation
5. Goblet cell loss and epithelial damage

## NF-κB Pathway

**Role in Dry Eye:**
- Master regulator of inflammation
- Activated by osmotic stress
- Upregulates pro-inflammatory genes
- Target for some treatments

**Activation Triggers:**
- Hyperosmolarity
- Mechanical stress
- Cytokines
- Bacterial products

## Anti-inflammatory Treatments

### Mechanisms

**Corticosteroids:**
- Broad anti-inflammatory
- Inhibit NF-κB pathway
- Reduce all inflammatory mediators

**Cyclosporine:**
- Inhibits T-cell activation
- Reduces cytokine production
- Increases tear production

**Lifitegrast:**
- Blocks LFA-1/ICAM-1 interaction
- Prevents T-cell adhesion
- Reduces inflammation

**Doxycycline:**
- Inhibits MMP production
- Anti-inflammatory at sub-antimicrobial doses
- Reduces cytokine production

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. MMP-9 is a key inflammatory marker
2. IL-1, IL-6, and TNF-α are major cytokines
3. Th17 cells are important in chronic dry eye
4. NF-κB is a master inflammatory pathway
5. Different treatments target different pathways
6. Inflammation perpetuates dry eye cycle
