# MGD Treatment Protocols

## Introduction

Meibomian gland dysfunction (MGD) is the leading cause of evaporative dry eye. A systematic, stepwise approach to treatment is essential for effective management. This guide outlines evidence-based treatment protocols ranging from conservative measures to advanced interventions.

## Treatment Hierarchy

### Level 1: Conservative Management

**Patient Education**
- Explanation of chronic nature of MGD
- Importance of treatment compliance
- Realistic expectations for improvement
- Identification and modification of risk factors

**Lid Hygiene**

*Warm Compresses:*
- **Purpose**: Liquefy meibum, open obstructed ducts
- **Temperature**: 40-45°C (optimal for meibum melting)
- **Duration**: 10-15 minutes, 1-2 times daily
- **Methods**:
  - Warm washcloth (cools quickly)
  - Commercial warm compress masks (more consistent)
  - Rice/bean bag devices
  - Heated eye masks

*Lid Massage:*
- **Purpose**: Express obstructed glands
- **Technique**:
  1. Apply warm compress first
  2. Use clean fingers or cotton tip
  3. Apply gentle pressure to tarsal plate
  4. Roll toward lid margin
  5. Upper lid: massage downward
  6. Lower lid: massage upward
- **Frequency**: After warm compress, 1-2 times daily

**Omega-3 Supplementation**
- **Mechanism**: Anti-inflammatory, improves meibum quality
- **Dosing**: 1000-2000 mg EPA + DHA daily
- **Duration**: 3-6 months for effect
- **Evidence**: Mixed results, may benefit selected patients

### Level 2: Pharmacological Treatment

**Topical Antibiotics**

*Azithromycin 1%:*
- **Mechanism**: Antibacterial, anti-inflammatory
- **Dosing**: 1 drop BID for 2 days, then daily for 4 weeks
- **Benefits**: Improves meibum quality, reduces symptoms

**Oral Antibiotics**

*Doxycycline:*
- **Mechanism**: Matrix metalloproteinase inhibition, anti-inflammatory
- **Dosing**:
  - Loading: 100 mg daily for 4-6 weeks
  - Maintenance: 40-50 mg daily
  - Low-dose: 20 mg twice daily (Oracea)
- **Duration**: 2-3 months minimum
- **Side Effects**: Photosensitivity, GI upset
- **Precautions**: Avoid in pregnancy, children under 8

### Level 3: In-Office Procedures

**Thermal Pulsation Therapy (LipiFlow)**

*Mechanism:*
- Applies heat to inner lid surface (42.5°C)
- Simultaneous pressure to outer lid
- Expresses glands during treatment

*Protocol:*
- Single 12-minute treatment
- Warmth phase + expression phase
- Repeat every 6-12 months as needed

**Intense Pulsed Light (IPL)**

*Mechanism:*
- Broad-spectrum light (500-1200 nm)
- Targets telangiectasia (abnormal vessels)
- Reduces inflammation

*Protocol:*
- 3-4 treatments, 2-4 weeks apart
- Protective shields required
- Combined with meibomian gland expression

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Warm compresses should be 40-45°C for 10-15 minutes
2. Doxycycline works via anti-inflammatory mechanism at low doses
3. LipiFlow applies heat at 42.5°C for 12 minutes
4. IPL targets abnormal blood vessels and inflammation
5. Treatment hierarchy progresses from conservative to invasive
6. Most patients need combination therapy
