# Staphylococcal Blepharitis

## Introduction

Staphylococcal blepharitis is a common form of anterior blepharitis caused by Staphylococcus aureus and coagulase-negative staphylococci. It is characterized by inflammation of the anterior lid margin with distinctive clinical features.

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- Foreign body sensation
- Burning and stinging
- Crusting of lashes
- Red, irritated eyes
- Worse in the morning

### Signs

**Lid Margin:**
- Hyperemia and telangiectasia
- Collarettes (fibrinous scales around lash bases)
- Madarosis (lash loss)
- Poliosis (whitening of lashes)
- Misdirected lashes (trichiasis)

**Conjunctiva:**
- Papillary conjunctivitis
- Hyperemia

**Cornea (in advanced cases):**
- Marginal keratitis
- Phlyctenulosis
- Punctate epithelial erosions

## Pathophysiology

**Bacterial Factors:**
- Toxin production (exotoxins)
- Lipase production (degrades meibum)
- Immune response to bacterial antigens

**Inflammatory Cascade:**
- Bacterial antigen triggers immune response
- Complement activation
- Neutrophil infiltration
- Release of inflammatory mediators

## Diagnosis

**Clinical Diagnosis Based On:**
- Characteristic appearance of collarettes
- Lid margin inflammation
- Symptoms consistent with blepharitis
- Exclusion of other causes

**Additional Testing (if needed):**
- Lid cultures (rarely needed)
- Lash microscopy for Demodex

## Treatment

### Lid Hygiene
- Warm compresses
- Lid scrubs with mild cleanser
- Mechanical removal of collarettes

### Topical Antibiotics

**First-Line:**
- Bacitracin ointment at bedtime
- Erythromycin ointment at bedtime
- Apply to lid margin after hygiene

**Second-Line:**
- Azithromycin 1% gel
- Fluoroquinolone drops/ointment

### Topical Corticosteroids
- Short course for acute inflammation
- Loteprednol or fluorometholone
- Monitor for complications

### Oral Antibiotics (Severe/Refractory)
- Doxycycline 100 mg daily
- Minocycline 50-100 mg daily

## Complications

**Local:**
- Chalazion formation
- Hordeolum
- Trichiasis
- Lid notching

**Ocular Surface:**
- Marginal keratitis
- Dry eye exacerbation
- Conjunctivitis

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Collarettes are pathognomonic for staphylococcal blepharitis
2. Worse symptoms in the morning
3. First-line treatment is lid hygiene + topical antibiotic
4. Can cause marginal keratitis
5. Chronic condition requiring long-term management
6. Associated with dry eye and MGD
