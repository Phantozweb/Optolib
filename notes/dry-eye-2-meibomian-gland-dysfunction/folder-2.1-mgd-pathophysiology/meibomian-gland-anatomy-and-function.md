# Meibomian Gland Anatomy and Function

## Introduction

Meibomian glands are holocrine sebaceous glands located within the tarsal plates of the eyelids. These specialized structures are essential for maintaining tear film stability and ocular surface health. Understanding their anatomy and function is fundamental to diagnosing and treating meibomian gland dysfunction (MGD).

## Gross Anatomy

### Location and Distribution

**Upper Eyelid:**
- Approximately 25-40 glands
- Arranged vertically within the tarsal plate
- Orifices located posterior to the gray line (mucocutaneous junction)
- Glands span the entire tarsal plate height

**Lower Eyelid:**
- Approximately 20-30 glands
- Shorter than upper lid glands (smaller tarsal plate)
- Similar arrangement to upper lid glands

### Structural Components

**Each meibomian gland consists of:**

1. **Central Duct (Ductule)**
   - Long central lumen running vertically
   - Lined by stratified squamous epithelium
   - Keratinized near the orifice
   - Conveys meibum to the lid margin

2. **Acini (Secretory Units)**
   - Grape-like clusters of secretory cells
   - Lateral to the central duct
   - Connected to duct via short ductules
   - Site of meibum synthesis and storage

3. **Orifice**
   - Opening at the lid margin
   - Located just anterior to the mucocutaneous junction
   - Approximately 0.1-0.5 mm in diameter
   - Visible with magnification

## Physiology

### Meibum Synthesis

**Lipid Composition:**
- **Wax Esters**: 40-45% (major component)
- **Cholesterol Esters**: 30-40%
- **Free Fatty Acids**: 5-10%
- **Phospholipids**: 5-10%
- **Free Cholesterol**: 2-5%

### Secretion Mechanism

**Holocrine Secretion:**
1. Lipid synthesis in acinar cells
2. Cell maturation and lipid accumulation
3. Cell death and release of contents
4. Lipid enters ductule
5. Transport to orifice
6. Delivery to tear film

**Regulation of Secretion:**

**Neural Control:**
- Parasympathetic: increases secretion
- Sympathetic: modulates secretion
- Sensory feedback from ocular surface

**Hormonal Influence:**
- Androgens: stimulate secretion
- Estrogens: complex effects
- Thyroid hormones: modulate function

### Meibum Properties

**Physical Characteristics:**
- **Melting Point**: 28-32°C (near body temperature)
- **State at Ocular Temperature**: Liquid-crystalline
- **Viscosity**: Affected by temperature and composition
- **Spreading**: Rapid (within one blink)

## Function in Tear Film

### Roles of Meibum

**1. Prevention of Evaporation**
- Creates hydrophobic barrier
- Reduces aqueous tear evaporation by up to 95%
- Critical for maintaining tear volume

**2. Tear Film Stability**
- Lowers surface tension
- Promotes even spreading
- Prevents tear film breakup

**3. Optical Function**
- Smooths corneal surface
- Enhances optical quality
- Reduces aberrations

**4. Barrier Function**
- Prevents skin lipids from contaminating tear film
- Blocks entry of environmental contaminants
- Contains antimicrobial components

**5. Lubrication**
- Reduces friction during blinking
- Protects lid margin epithelium
- Facilitates smooth lid movement

## Clinical Relevance for Exams

**Key Points to Remember:**

1. Upper lid has more glands (25-40) than lower lid (20-30)
2. Meibum is primarily wax esters and cholesterol esters
3. Melting point of meibum is 28-32°C
4. Blinking mechanically expresses meibum
5. Lipid layer reduces evaporation by up to 95%
6. Androgens stimulate meibomian gland function

## Conclusion

Understanding meibomian gland anatomy and physiology is essential for diagnosing and treating MGD, the most common cause of evaporative dry eye. The complex structure and function of these glands make them vulnerable to dysfunction, with significant impact on ocular surface health.
