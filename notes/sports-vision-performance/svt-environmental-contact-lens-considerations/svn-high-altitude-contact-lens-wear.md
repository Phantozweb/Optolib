# High Altitude Contact Lens Wear

## Introduction

Contact lenses represent a preferred vision correction option for many athletes, offering advantages over spectacles in terms of field of view, stability during activity, and compatibility with protective equipment. This comprehensive guide addresses contact lens considerations for athletes, focusing on environmental contact lens considerations within the context of contact lenses for athletes.

## Advantages of Contact Lenses for Athletes

### Optical Benefits
- **Unobstructed Peripheral Vision**: Full field of view without spectacle frame limitations
- **Stable Vision**: No movement or displacement during physical activity
- **Reduced Distortions**: Elimination of spectacle lens aberrations in peripheral gaze
- **Consistent Optical Correction**: Vision remains stable regardless of head position or movement
- **No Fogging**: Eliminates issues with temperature changes and perspiration

### Practical Advantages
- Compatible with protective eyewear and helmets
- No interference with sports equipment
- Reduced risk of injury from broken frames or lenses
- Improved comfort during prolonged activity
- Enhanced performance in adverse weather conditions

## Contact Lens Selection

### Lens Material Considerations

**Silicone Hydrogel Lenses:**
- High oxygen transmissibility
- Extended wear options
- Reduced risk of hypoxia-related complications
- Suitable for long-duration activities
- Preferred for most athletic applications

**Hydrogel Lenses:**
- Lower oxygen transmission
- Higher water content options
- May dry faster in windy conditions
- Generally lower cost
- Suitable for shorter duration activities

**Rigid Gas Permeable (RGP) Lenses:**
- Superior optics for high astigmatism
- Stable vision
- Require adaptation period
- Risk of dislodgement during impact
- May not be ideal for contact sports

### Lens Modality Options

**Daily Disposable Lenses:**
- Most hygienic option for athletes
- No cleaning or storage required
- Ideal for intermittent athletes
- Emergency backup-friendly
- Higher per-lens cost but lower overall expense

**Frequent Replacement (2-week/1-month):**
- Cost-effective for regular wearers
- Established comfort and fit
- Requires proper hygiene compliance
- Good option for training and competition

**Extended Wear Lenses:**
- Approved for overnight wear
- Reduces handling frequency
- Higher infection risk
- May be appropriate for specific situations
- Requires careful patient selection

## Sport-Specific Considerations

### Water Sports
- **Swimming**: Generally discouraged due to infection risk (Acanthamoeba)
- **Water Polo**: Contact lenses under goggles only
- **Surfing**: Daily disposables with tight-fitting goggles
- **Recommendations**: Consider prescription goggles; never expose lenses to water

### Ball and Racket Sports
- Daily disposables preferred
- Consider UV protection
- Stable fit essential
- Backup lenses should be available

### Combat Sports
- Eye protection mandatory with contact lenses
- High risk of lens displacement
- Daily disposables recommended
- Immediate replacement capability

### Winter Sports
- Cold, dry conditions increase lens drying
- UV protection essential
- Consider larger diameter lenses
- Sunglasses over contacts recommended

### Motor Sports
- Stable vision critical
- Wind protection needed
- Extended wear may be considered
- Hydration important

## Special Vision Correction Challenges

### High Astigmatism
- Toric contact lenses essential
- Consider RGP for highest correction
- Stability of lens orientation critical
- Digital toric designs available

### Presbyopia
- Multifocal contact lens options
- Monovision correction
- Modified monovision approaches
- May need different solutions for training vs. competition

### Keratoconus
- Specialized lens fitting required
- Scleral lenses may be optimal
- Sports-specific considerations
- Close monitoring essential

## Environmental Considerations

### Dust and Debris
- Daily disposables recommended
- Protective eyewear essential
- Immediate removal if contaminated
- Backup lenses available

### Temperature Extremes
- Heat: Increased dehydration and lens drying
- Cold: Reduced tear production
- Altitude: Hypoxia concerns; consider higher Dk lenses

### Humidity Variations
- Low humidity: Increased lens drying
- High humidity: Generally comfortable
- Consider rewetting drops

## Pre-Competition Protocols

### Lens Insertion Timeline
- Insert lenses at least 30 minutes before activity
- Allow lens to stabilize
- Verify clear, comfortable vision
- Test lens position with sport-specific movements

### Emergency Preparedness
- Carry backup lenses
- Include lens case and solution
- Have spectacles available
- Know how to handle complications

## Hygiene Protocols for Athletes

### Hand Hygiene
- Wash hands thoroughly before handling lenses
- Use lint-free towels for drying
- Carry hand sanitizer for field situations
- Avoid touching lenses during activity

### Lens Care
- Follow replacement schedule strictly
- Clean storage case regularly
- Use fresh solution daily
- Never expose lenses to water

## Problem-Solving Common Issues

### Lens Displacement During Activity
- Larger diameter lenses may help
- Confirm proper fit
- Consider different lens design
- Practice repositioning technique

### Dryness and Discomfort
- Increase hydration
- Use rewetting drops approved for contact lenses
- Consider different lens material
- Evaluate fit and prescription

### Visual Fluctuation
- Verify stable fit
- Check for lens deposits
- Evaluate tear film
- Consider lens replacement

## Clinical Pearls

1. Daily disposable lenses offer the best combination of convenience, hygiene, and performance
2. Always have backup lenses and spectacles available
3. Fit athletes with sport-specific demands in mind
4. Educate thoroughly on hygiene and emergency procedures
5. Consider environmental conditions when selecting lenses
6. Document fitting decisions for future reference

## Conclusion

Contact lenses provide excellent vision correction for athletes when properly selected, fitted, and maintained. By understanding sport-specific demands and environmental challenges, eye care professionals can optimize visual performance while ensuring ocular health and safety.
