# Esotropia in Children

## Introduction

Esotropia, or inward deviation of the eyes, is the most common form of strabismus in children. Proper classification, evaluation, and management are essential for optimal visual outcomes and binocular function.

## Classification

### Types of Esotropia

**1. Congenital/Infantile Esotropia**
- Onset before 6 months of age
- Large angle (typically 30-50 PD)
- Cross-fixation pattern
- May have dissociated vertical deviation (DVD)
- Requires surgical intervention

**2. Accommodative Esotropia**
- Refractive: Fully corrected with hyperopic glasses
- High AC/A: Greater deviation at near
- Partial: Residual deviation with glasses
- Onset typically 2-4 years

**3. Acquired Non-accommodative**
- Later onset
- May indicate neurological pathology
- Requires neuroimaging in some cases

**4. Sensory Esotropia**
- Due to vision loss in one eye
- Childhood onset typically leads to esotropia
- Adult onset may cause exotropia

## Clinical Features

### Infantile Esotropia

**Characteristics:**
- Constant, large angle deviation
- Alternating cross-fixation
- May appear to have bilateral abduction weakness
- DVD and latent nystagmus common
- Inferior oblique overaction common

### Accommodative Esotropia

**Characteristics:**
- Intermittent onset, becomes constant
- Smaller angle than infantile
- Associated with hyperopia
- Worse at near (if high AC/A)
- May develop amblyopia

## Evaluation

### Assessment Protocol

1. **History:**
   - Age of onset
   - Frequency and variability
   - Glasses history
   - Family history

2. **Visual Acuity:**
   - Age-appropriate testing
   - Fixation preference

3. **Motor Assessment:**
   - Cover testing (distance and near)
   - Prism measurement
   - Versions and ductions

4. **Refraction:**
   - Cycloplegic essential
   - Often +3.00 to +6.00 D

5. **AC/A Calculation:**
   - Heterophoria method
   - Lens gradient method

## Management

### Infantile Esotropia

**Treatment:**
- Surgical correction (typically bilateral medial rectus recession)
- Optimal timing: 6-12 months for binocularity
- May require multiple surgeries
- Post-operative vision therapy consideration

### Accommodative Esotropia

**Treatment:**
1. **Refractive:**
   - Full hyperopic correction
   - Single vision lenses initially

2. **High AC/A:**
   - Bifocals if significant near deviation
   - Executive or flat-top style
   - Consider MiSight or multifocal contacts

3. **Partially Accommodative:**
   - Glasses for accommodative component
   - Surgery for residual deviation

1. Classification of esotropia types
2. Accommodative vs. infantile differentiation
3. AC/A calculation methods
4. Treatment sequencing
5. Bifocal indications

## Summary

Esotropia encompasses multiple etiologies requiring different management approaches. Accurate classification and measurement guide appropriate treatment selection.
