# Patching Protocols for Amblyopia

## Introduction

Occlusion therapy remains a cornerstone of amblyopia treatment. Understanding evidence-based patching protocols, compliance strategies, and monitoring approaches is essential for optimal outcomes.

## Evidence-Based Dosing

### PEDIG Studies

**ATS Studies (Amblyopia Treatment Studies):**

| Severity | Age | Patching Duration | Atropine Alternative |
|----------|-----|-------------------|---------------------|
| Moderate (20/40-20/80) | 3-7 years | 2 hours/day | Weekend atropine |
| Severe (20/100-20/400) | 3-7 years | 6 hours/day | Daily atropine |
| Moderate | 7-12 years | 2-6 hours/day | Daily atropine |
| Severe | 7-12 years | 6+ hours/day | Daily atropine |

## Implementation

### Starting Treatment

**Steps:**
1. Ensure optimal optical correction
2. Wait 4-6 weeks for refractive adaptation
3. Start with prescribed patching hours
4. Monitor at 4-6 week intervals
5. Adjust based on response

### Patch Types

**Options:**
- Adhesive patches (preferred)
- Pirate patches (less reliable)
- Bangerter filters (partial occlusion)
- Luminator/eyedrop method

## Compliance Strategies

### Improving Adherence

**For Children:**
- Decorative patches with characters
- Reward charts and stickers
- Patching during preferred activities
- Consistent daily schedule
- Parent education on importance

**For Parents:**
- Clear explanation of condition
- Written instructions
- Demonstration of patch application
- Contact information for concerns
- Follow-up appointment scheduling

## Monitoring

### Follow-up Schedule

| Treatment Phase | Follow-up Interval |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Initial treatment | 4-6 weeks |
| Active improvement | 4-8 weeks |
| Plateau reached | 2-3 months |
| Maintenance/stopping | 3-6 months |

### Success Criteria

**Treatment Endpoints:**
- Visual acuity equal between eyes
- Stable acuity for 3 consecutive visits
- Maximum improvement plateau
- 20/20 or better achieved

## Complications

### Potential Issues

1. **Reverse Amblyopia:**
   - Occlusion amblyopia of sound eye
   - More common with full-time patching
   - Monitor closely

2. **Skin Irritation:**
   - Adhesive allergy
   - Rotation of patch brands
   - Barrier creams

3. **Psychological Impact:**
   - Behavioral resistance
   - Social stigma
   - Consider atropine alternative

## Licensing Exam Points

1. ATS study dosing recommendations
2. Monitoring intervals
3. Recognition of occlusion amblyopia
4. Treatment endpoints

## Summary

Evidence-based patching protocols optimize outcomes while minimizing adverse effects. Regular monitoring and compliance strategies are essential for successful amblyopia treatment.
