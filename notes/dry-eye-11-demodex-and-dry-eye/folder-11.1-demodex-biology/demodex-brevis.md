# Demodex-Associated Blepharitis

## Introduction

Demodex mites are common ectoparasites that can infest the eyelids and contribute to blepharitis. Two species affect humans: Demodex folliculorum (in lash follicles) and Demodex brevis (in meibomian glands).

## Demodex Species

### Demodex Folliculorum
- Lives in eyelash follicles
- More commonly associated with cylindrical dandruff
- Can cause direct damage to follicles

### Demodex Brevis
- Lives in meibomian glands
- Deeper infestation
- Can affect meibum quality

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- Itching
- Burning
- Foreign body sensation
- Lid margin irritation

### Signs
- **Cylindrical Dandruff**: Pathognomonic finding
  - Sleeves of debris around lash bases
  - Clear, waxy appearance
  - Mites visible at lash base

**Other Signs:**
- Lid margin hyperemia
- Madarosis
- Lid margin thickening
- Meibomian gland dysfunction

## Diagnosis

### Cylindrical Dandruff Examination
- Slit lamp examination
- Look for sleeves around lash bases
- Location: primarily upper lid

### Epilation and Microscopy
- Epilate affected lashes
- Place on slide with oil
- Examine under microscope
- Count mites (>1 mite/lash is significant)

### In Vivo Confocal Microscopy
- Can visualize mites in follicles
- Research tool primarily

## Treatment

### Tea Tree Oil

**Mechanism:**
- Scabicidal properties
- Terminates mites
- Improves symptoms

**Concentrations:**
- 50% TTO in-office treatment
- Lower concentrations for home use
- Cliradex (4-terpineol isolate)

**Protocol:**
1. Weekly in-office 50% TTO scrubs
2. Daily home cleanser with TTO
3. Continue for 4-6 weeks (mite life cycle)

### Ivermectin

**Topical (Soolantra 1%):**
- Applied to affected skin
- BID for several months
- Good for facial rosacea too

**Oral:**
- 200 μg/kg single dose
- May repeat in 1-2 weeks
- Reserved for severe cases

### Hypochlorous Acid
- Antimicrobial properties
- Less irritating than TTO
- Adjunctive treatment

## Management Protocol

1. **Diagnosis**: Confirm with cylindrical dandruff visualization
2. **Treatment**: TTO-based products + lid hygiene
3. **Duration**: At least 4-6 weeks (cover mite life cycle)
4. **Maintenance**: Continue lid hygiene to prevent recurrence
5. **Follow-up**: Re-evaluate at 6 weeks

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Cylindrical dandruff is pathognomonic for Demodex
2. Two species: folliculorum (follicles) and brevis (glands)
3. Tea tree oil is primary treatment
4. Treatment must cover 4-6 week life cycle
5. Associated with MGD and ocular rosacea
6. Microscopic examination confirms diagnosis
