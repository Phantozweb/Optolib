# Contact Lens-Related Dry Eye

## Introduction

Contact lens-induced dry eye (CLIDE) is a common problem affecting contact lens wearers. Understanding the mechanisms and management strategies is essential for successful contact lens practice.

## Mechanisms

### Tear Film Alterations
- Pre-lens tear film is less stable
- Increased evaporation
- Altered tear composition
- Reduced tear volume under lens

### Mechanical Factors
- Reduced blink rate during lens wear
- Incomplete blinking
- Lens dehydration
- Friction between lens and ocular surface

### Material Factors
- Water content of lens
- Surface wettability
- Oxygen permeability
- Lens deposits

## CLIDE Definition and Diagnosis

**Definition:**
Contact lens-induced dry eye is diagnosed when a contact lens wearer experiences dry eye symptoms specifically during lens wear, with or without clinical signs.

**Diagnostic Criteria:**
- Symptoms worse during lens wear
- Symptoms improve when lenses removed
- May have reduced TBUT with lenses
- Normal or reduced tear production
- Signs may be mild compared to symptoms

## Risk Factors

**Patient Factors:**
- Female sex
- Pre-existing dry eye
- Systemic medications
- Environmental exposure
- Digital device use

**Lens Factors:**
- High water content materials
- Silicone hydrogel materials (some)
- Deposited lenses
- Poor fit

**Modality Factors:**
- Extended wear
- Infrequent replacement
- Inadequate cleaning

## Management Strategies

### Lens Selection

**Material Considerations:**
- Lower water content may be better
- Improved surface treatments
- Daily disposables preferred

**Fit Considerations:**
- Adequate movement
- Minimal edge lift
- Good centration

### Rewetting Drops

**Options:**
- Preservative-free preferred
- Compatible with lens material
- May need frequent use

**Timing:**
- Before symptoms develop
- During prolonged wear
- Before and after digital device use

### Lens Care

**Solutions:**
- Preservative-sensitive patients: hydrogen peroxide
- Match solution to lens material
- Consider rewetting solution additives

### Environmental Management

- Humidified environments
- Breaks from digital devices
- Proper lighting
- Adequate hydration

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. CLIDE is common and often underdiagnosed
2. Pre-lens tear film is less stable than pre-corneal
3. Daily disposables often improve symptoms
4. Preservative-free rewetting drops preferred
5. Rule out pre-existing dry eye before fitting
6. Lens material and fit affect dryness risk
