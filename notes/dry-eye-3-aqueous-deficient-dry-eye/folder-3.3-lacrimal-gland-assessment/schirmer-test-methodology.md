# Schirmer Test Methodology

## Introduction

The Schirmer test is a standard method for assessing tear production. It measures the wetting of a filter paper strip placed in the inferior conjunctival sac over a specified time period.

## Types of Schirmer Tests

### Schirmer I (Without Anesthesia)
- Measures total tear secretion (basal + reflex)
- Less specific for basal secretion
- Normal: >15 mm in 5 minutes

### Schirmer I (With Anesthesia)
- Measures basal tear secretion
- Anesthetic eliminates reflex tearing
- Normal: >10 mm in 5 minutes
- Abnormal: ≤5 mm in 5 minutes

## Procedure

### Materials
- Schirmer strips (5 x 35 mm filter paper)
- Topical anesthetic (proparacaine)
- Timer

### Steps
1. Apply anesthetic drop (if with anesthesia)
2. Wait 1 minute for effect
3. Remove excess fluid with tissue
4. Bend strip at notch (5 mm from end)
5. Place hooked end in inferior conjunctival sac
6. Position at junction of middle and lateral thirds
7. Avoid touching cornea
8. Patient closes eyes gently
9. Wait 5 minutes
10. Remove strip and measure wetting

## Interpretation

| Wetting Length | Interpretation |
|----------------|----------------|
| >15 mm | Normal |
| 10-15 mm | Borderline |
| 5-10 mm | Mild-moderate aqueous deficiency |
| <5 mm | Severe aqueous deficiency |

## Limitations

1. Variable reproducibility
2. Affected by ambient conditions
3. Patient discomfort
4. Reflex tearing may affect results
5. Not specific for lacrimal gland function

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Schirmer with anesthesia measures basal secretion
2. Normal is >10 mm with anesthesia, >15 mm without
3. ≤5 mm indicates severe aqueous deficiency
4. Place strip at junction of middle and lateral thirds
5. Wait 5 minutes for measurement
6. Consider variability in interpretation
