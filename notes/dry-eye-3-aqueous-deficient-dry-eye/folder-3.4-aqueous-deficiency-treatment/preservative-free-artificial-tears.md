# Artificial Tear Formulations

## Introduction

Artificial tears are the first-line treatment for dry eye disease. Understanding the different formulations, their properties, and appropriate selection is essential for effective patient management.

## Types of Artificial Tears

### By Composition

**Aqueous-Based:**
- Supplement tear volume
- Best for aqueous deficiency
- Examples: Systane, Refresh Tears, TheraTears

**Lipid-Based (Emulsions):**
- Replace deficient lipid layer
- Best for evaporative dry eye
- Examples: Systane Balance, Refresh Optive Advanced, Soothe XP

**Carbomer Gels:**
- Longer duration
- May blur vision
- Examples: Genteal Gel, Refresh Liquigel

### By Preservative Status

**Preserved:**
- Benzalkonium chloride (BAK) most common
- Multi-dose bottles
- May cause toxicity with frequent use

**Preservative-Free:**
- Single-use vials
- Recommended for frequent use (>4x daily)
- Examples: Refresh Plus, TheraTears PF

**Vanishing Preservatives:**
- Break down upon contact with eye
- Multi-dose convenience
- Examples: Systane Complete, Refresh Optive

## Active Ingredients

**Viscosity Agents:**
- Carboxymethylcellulose (CMC)
- Hydroxypropyl methylcellulose (HPMC)
- Polyethylene glycol (PEG)
- Propylene glycol
- Hyaluronic acid

**Lipids:**
- Castor oil
- Mineral oil
- Phospholipids

**Electrolytes:**
- Potassium chloride
- Sodium chloride
- Calcium chloride

## Selection Guidelines

### Based on Dry Eye Type

**Aqueous Deficiency:**
- High-viscosity aqueous formulations
- Electrolyte-containing products
- May need frequent application

**Evaporative/MGD:**
- Lipid-containing emulsions
- Formulations with castor oil
- Consider gel formulations

**Mixed Mechanism:**
- Combination products
- Multiple formulations may be needed

### Based on Severity

**Mild:**
- Over-the-counter preserved tears
- As needed use

**Moderate:**
- Preservative-free formulations
- Scheduled dosing (4x daily minimum)

**Severe:**
- Gel formulations at night
- Preservative-free throughout day
- Consider serum tears

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Use preservative-free for frequent use (>4x daily)
2. Lipid-based tears for evaporative dry eye
3. Gels provide longer duration but blur vision
4. Hyaluronic acid has good retention properties
5. Match formulation to dry eye subtype
6. Schedule dosing rather than PRN for moderate-severe
