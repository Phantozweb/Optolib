# Autologous Serum Eye Drops

## Introduction

Autologous serum eye drops (ASED) are prepared from a patient's own blood serum and used for severe ocular surface disease. They contain growth factors and nutrients that promote epithelial healing and reduce inflammation.

## Composition

**Components in Serum:**
- Epidermal growth factor (EGF)
- Nerve growth factor (NGF)
- Fibronectin
- Vitamin A
- Lysozyme
- IgG antibodies
- Various cytokines

**Similarity to Natural Tears:**
- pH and osmolality match natural tears
- Contains growth factors not in artificial tears
- Promotes epithelial proliferation and migration

## Indications

**Primary Indications:**
- Severe dry eye unresponsive to conventional treatment
- Persistent corneal epithelial defects
- Neurotrophic keratopathy
- Sjögren's syndrome with severe dry eye
- Graft-versus-host disease

**Other Uses:**
- Ocular surface burns
- Recurrent corneal erosion
- Post-surgical healing

## Preparation

**Process:**
1. Draw 50-100 mL of patient's blood
2. Centrifuge to separate serum
3. Dilute to 20% or 50% with saline
4. Aliquot into sterile dropper bottles
5. Freeze until use
6. Thaw individual bottles as needed

**Storage:**
- Frozen: up to 3 months
- Thawed (refrigerated): up to 7 days
- Room temperature: use immediately

## Administration

**Dosing:**
- Typically every 1-2 hours while awake
- Taper as condition improves
- May be used indefinitely for chronic conditions

**Concentration:**
- 20% most commonly used
- 50% for more severe cases
- 100% occasionally used

## Efficacy

**Benefits:**
- Promotes epithelial healing
- Reduces inflammation
- Improves symptoms
- May reduce need for other medications

**Limitations:**
- Requires venipuncture
- Storage requirements
- Cost and access issues
- Not always covered by insurance

## Contraindications

- Active systemic infection
- Inability to give blood
- Severe anemia
- Blood-borne diseases (HIV, hepatitis)

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Serum contains growth factors not in artificial tears
2. Indicated for severe dry eye unresponsive to treatment
3. Typically 20% concentration
4. Requires blood draw and processing
5. Frozen storage up to 3 months
6. Not available commercially - compounding pharmacy
