# Atropine for Myopia Control

## Introduction

Low-dose atropine has emerged as a first-line pharmacological intervention for myopia control. Understanding dosing, efficacy, safety, and monitoring is essential for clinical practice.

## Mechanism of Action

### Proposed Mechanisms

**Theories:**
1. Muscarinic receptor antagonism in sclera
2. Dopamine modulation
3. Reduced accommodation (less relevant at low doses)
4. Direct scleral effects on remodeling
5. Potential retinal mechanisms

## Clinical Evidence

### ATOM Studies

**ATOM 1 (2006):**
- 1% atropine vs placebo
- 77% reduction in progression
- Significant side effects (photophobia, near blur)
- Rebound effect after cessation

**ATOM 2 (2012):**
- Compared 0.5%, 0.1%, 0.01%
- 0.01%: Best efficacy-side effect profile
- 50% reduction in progression
- Minimal rebound

**ATOM 3 (2020):**
- Combination therapy study
- Atropine + orthokeratology
- Enhanced efficacy

### Dosing Comparison

| Concentration | Efficacy | Side Effects | Rebound |
|--------------|----------|--------------|---------|
| 1.0% | Highest | Significant | Moderate |
| 0.5% | High | Moderate | Moderate |
| 0.1% | Moderate | Minimal | Mild |
| 0.025% | Moderate | Minimal | Minimal |
| 0.01% | Good | Negligible | Minimal |

## Clinical Protocol

### Starting Treatment

**Patient Selection:**
- Progressive myopia
- Age 5-16 years
- Informed consent
- Realistic expectations

**Baseline Assessment:**
1. Cycloplegic refraction
2. Best-corrected visual acuity
3. Axial length measurement
4. Anterior segment examination
5. Intraocular pressure
6. Pupil size measurement

**Initiation:**
- Start with 0.01% nightly
- One drop to each eye at bedtime
- Wash hands before application
- Nasolacrimal occlusion for 2 minutes

### Monitoring Schedule

| Timepoint | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| 1 month | Tolerance check, side effects |
| 3 months | Visual acuity, near vision |
| 6 months | Full assessment with cycloplegia |
| 12 months | Comprehensive evaluation |

## Side Effects

### Low-Dose (0.01%) Profile

**Minimal Effects:**
- Slight pupil dilation (1-2mm)
- Minimal near vision impact
- Rare photophobia
- Excellent tolerance

### Higher Concentration Effects

**More Common:**
- Significant mydriasis
- Accommodative paresis
- Near blur
- Photophobia requiring sunglasses
- Allergic reactions (rare)

## Efficacy Enhancement

### When 0.01% Insufficient

**Options:**
1. Increase concentration (0.025%, 0.05%)
2. Add combination therapy
3. Switch to alternative intervention

### Combination Strategies

**Evidence-Based Combinations:**
- Atropine + orthokeratology
- Atropine + specialty lenses
- Atropine + soft multifocal contacts

## Duration of Treatment

### Treatment Timeline

**Typical Duration:**
- Minimum 2 years
- Often until late teens
- Consider tapering when stable

**Stopping Criteria:**
- Age >16 with stability
- <0.25 D progression over 2 years
- Patient choice

### Rebound Phenomenon

**Characteristics:**
- More common with higher concentrations
- Accelerated progression after stopping
- Mitigated by slow tapering
- Less with 0.01%

## Special Considerations

### Contraindications

**Relative:**
- Down syndrome (accommodative issues)
- Current eye infection
- Known hypersensitivity

**Absolute:**
- Narrow angle glaucoma
- Previous severe reaction

### Compounding Issues

**Challenges:**
- Availability varies by region
- Preservative-free preferred
- Storage requirements
- Expiration dating
- Cost considerations
