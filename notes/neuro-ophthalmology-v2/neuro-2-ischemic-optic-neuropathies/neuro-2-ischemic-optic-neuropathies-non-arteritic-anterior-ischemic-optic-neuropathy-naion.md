# Non-Arteritic Anterior Ischemic Optic Neuropathy (NAION): Comprehensive Guide

## Introduction

NAION is the most common acute optic neuropathy in patients over 50 years of age. It results from ischemia of the optic nerve head due to compromise of the short posterior ciliary arteries.

## Risk Factors

### Ocular Factors
- Small crowded optic disc ("disc at risk")
- No physiologic cup
- Optic disc drusen

### Systemic Factors
- Hypertension
- Diabetes mellitus
- Hyperlipidemia
- Sleep apnea
- Nocturnal hypotension

## Clinical Presentation

### Acute Phase
- Sudden, painless vision loss
- Usually unilateral initially
- No pain with eye movement
- Altitudinal visual field defect (inferior most common)
- Optic disc swelling with peripapillary hemorrhages
- Relative afferent pupillary defect

### Visual Field Defects
- Altitudinal (50%)
- Arcuate
- Central scotoma
- Diffuse

## Fundus Findings

### Acute
- Diffuse or segmental disc edema
- Peripapillary hemorrhages
- Hyperemic disc
- No cilioretinal artery sparing typically

### Chronic
- Optic disc pallor
- Segmental atrophy
- Upper or lower rim thinning

## Diagnosis

### Clinical Diagnosis
- Based on characteristic presentation
- No specific confirmatory test

### Investigations
- ESR and CRP (to exclude GCA)
- Consider vascular risk factor evaluation
- MRI not routinely needed

## Treatment

### Current Options
- No proven effective treatment
- Address vascular risk factors
- Avoid nocturnal hypotension
- Consider aspirin for second eye protection

### Ischemic Optic Neuropathy Decompression Trial (IONDT)
- Optic nerve decompression NOT effective
- May cause harm
- 42.7% spontaneous improvement

## Prognosis

### Visual Outcome
- Variable, often stable
- Some spontaneous improvement possible
- Risk to fellow eye: 15-20% over 5 years

## Examination Points

**Key Facts for Licensing Exams:**
1. Most common acute optic neuropathy >50 years
2. Disc at risk: small cup-to-disc ratio
3. Altitudinal field defect most common
4. No proven treatment
5. ESR/CRP to rule out GCA
