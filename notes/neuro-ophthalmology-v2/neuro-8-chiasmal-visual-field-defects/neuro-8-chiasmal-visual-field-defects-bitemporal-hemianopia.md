# Bitemporal Hemianopia: Comprehensive Guide

## Introduction

Bitemporal hemianopia is a visual field defect involving the temporal visual fields of both eyes. It is the classic field defect seen with chiasmal lesions and represents one of the most important localizing signs in neuro-ophthalmology.

## Anatomical Basis

### Optic Chiasm Organization
- Nasal retinal fibers cross at chiasm
- Temporal retinal fibers remain uncrossed
- Crossing nasal fibers represent temporal visual fields
- Therefore, chiasmal compression → bitemporal defect

### Fiber Arrangement
- Inferior nasal fibers cross anteriorly (ventral chiasm)
- Superior nasal fibers cross posteriorly (dorsal chiasm)
- Macular fibers cross throughout the chiasm

## Etiology

### Most Common Causes
1. **Pituitary adenoma** (most common)
2. **Craniopharyngioma**
3. **Meningioma** (tuberculum sellae)
4. **Aneurysm** (anterior communicating)
5. **Metastatic tumors**

### Less Common
- Hypothalamic glioma
- Third ventricle tumors
- Chiasmatic astrocytoma
- Radiation damage
- Trauma

## Visual Field Patterns

### Classic Bitemporal Hemianopia
- Complete temporal field loss both eyes
- Midline respects vertical meridian
- Indicates central chiasmal compression

### Incomplete Patterns

**Bitemporal Hemianopic Scotoma:**
- Central involvement only
- Macular fibers affected
- Often early finding

**Superior Bitemporal Hemianopia:**
- Inferior nasal fibers affected first
- Lesion compressing from below
- Typical of pituitary adenoma

**Junctional Scotoma:**
- Central scotoma one eye
- Superior temporal defect other eye
- Anterior chiasmal involvement

## Associated Symptoms

### Visual
- Decreased visual acuity (if advanced)
- Visual field loss (may not notice initially)
- Diplopia (if cavernous sinus involved)

### Non-Visual
- Headache
- Hormonal disturbances (pituitary)
- Diabetes insipidus
- Growth abnormalities

## Diagnosis

### Visual Field Testing
- Automated perimetry (Humphrey)
- Goldmann kinetic perimetry
- Confrontation fields (screening)

### Neuroimaging
- **MRI with contrast:** Gold standard
- CT for bony detail
- Visualize pituitary and suprasellar region

### Endocrine Evaluation
- Hormone levels
- Pituitary function tests
- Endocrinology consultation

## Management

### Treatment Depends on Etiology

**Pituitary Adenoma:**
- Transsphenoidal surgery
- Medical therapy (prolactinoma)
- Radiation therapy

**Meningioma:**
- Surgical resection
- Radiation therapy

**Aneurysm:**
- Surgical clipping
- Endovascular coiling

### Visual Monitoring
- Serial visual fields
- Post-surgical recovery assessment
- Long-term follow-up

## Prognosis

### Visual Recovery
- Depends on duration and severity
- Recovery possible after surgical decompression
- Some field defects may be permanent

## Examination Points

**Key Facts for Licensing Exams:**
1. Bitemporal hemianopia = chiasmal lesion until proven otherwise
2. Pituitary adenoma most common cause
3. Superior field affected first with pituitary lesions
4. MRI essential for diagnosis
5. Junctional scotoma = anterior chiasmal involvement

**Clinical Pearl:**
Patient may not notice bitemporal hemianopia because:
- Temporal fields overlap minimally
- Both eyes affected symmetrically
- No diplopia or functional deficit initially
