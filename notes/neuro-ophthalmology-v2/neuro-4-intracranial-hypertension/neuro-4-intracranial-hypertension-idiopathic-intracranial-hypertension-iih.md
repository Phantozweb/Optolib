# Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension (IIH): Comprehensive Guide

## Introduction

Idiopathic intracranial hypertension (IIH), formerly called pseudotumor cerebri, is a disorder of increased intracranial pressure without identifiable cause. It predominantly affects obese women of childbearing age.

## Diagnostic Criteria (Modified Dandy Criteria)

### Essential Features
1. Signs and symptoms of increased ICP
2. No localizing neurologic signs (except CN VI palsy)
3. Normal neuroimaging (except empty sella)
4. Normal CSF composition
5. Elevated opening pressure (>25 cm H2O)

## Epidemiology

### Demographics
- Female:Male ratio 8:1
- Mean age: 30 years
- Strong association with obesity (90%+)
- Incidence increasing with obesity epidemic

## Clinical Presentation

### Symptoms
- **Headache:** Present in 90%+
- **Pulsatile tinnitus:** 60%
- **Transient visual obscurations:** 70%
- **Visual loss:** 30-70%
- **Nausea/vomiting:** 40%

### Signs
- Papilledema (bilateral, usually symmetric)
- Sixth nerve palsy (false localizing sign)
- Visual field defects
- Optic atrophy (late)

## Papilledema Features

### Frisen Grading
- **Grade 0:** Normal disc
- **Grade 1:** C-shaped edema
- **Grade 2:** Circumferential edema
- **Grade 3:** Elevation with obscured vessels
- **Grade 4:** Complete obscuration of vessels

## Visual Field Defects

### Common Patterns
- Enlarged blind spot (most common)
- Inferior nasal field loss
- Generalized depression
- Arcuate scotomas

## Neuroimaging

### MRI Findings
- Empty sella
- Flattening of posterior globes
- Distension of perioptic subarachnoid space
- Transverse sinus stenosis

### MRV
- May show venous sinus stenosis
- Rules out venous sinus thrombosis

## Lumbar Puncture

### Technique
- Lateral decubitus position
- Legs extended
- Normal opening pressure: <20 cm H2O
- IIH: >25 cm H2O (women), >28 cm H2O (men)

## Treatment

### Medical Management
- **Acetazolamide:** First-line, 1-4 g/day
- **Weight loss:** Essential
- **Furosemide:** Adjunct
- **Topiramate:** Alternative

### Surgical Options
- **Optic nerve sheath fenestration:** For vision-threatening papilledema
- **CSF shunting:** VP or LP shunt
- **Venous sinus stenting:** For selected cases

## Monitoring

### Follow-up Schedule
- Visual acuity
- Visual fields
- OCT (RNFL thickness)
- Fundus photography

## Examination Points

**High-Yield Facts:**
1. Obese women of childbearing age
2. Headache + papilledema + normal MRI
3. Opening pressure >25 cm H2O
4. Acetazolamide first-line treatment
5. Monitor visual fields closely
