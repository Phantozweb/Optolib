# Cyclosporine Ophthalmic Emulsion

## Introduction

Cyclosporine ophthalmic emulsion (Restasis, generic formulations) is an immunomodulatory agent used for the treatment of dry eye disease. It addresses the inflammatory component of dry eye pathophysiology.

## Mechanism of Action

**Immunomodulatory Effects:**
- Inhibits calcineurin
- Reduces T-cell activation and proliferation
- Decreases pro-inflammatory cytokines
- Increases tear production

**Effects on Ocular Surface:**
- Reduces inflammatory markers
- Improves goblet cell density
- Decreases conjunctival squamous metaplasia
- May improve meibomian gland function

## Available Formulations

### Restasis (0.05%)
- Original FDA-approved formulation
- Multi-dose bottle
- Requires shaking before use
- Emulsion vehicle

### Restasis MultiDose
- Single-use vials
- Preservative-free
- No shaking required

### Generic Cyclosporine 0.05%
- Various manufacturers
- May have different vehicles
- Bioequivalence established

### Cequa (Cyclosporine 0.09%)
- Higher concentration
- Nanomicellar formulation
- Enhanced penetration

### Klarity-C (Cyclosporine 0.09%)
- Similar formulation to Cequa

## Clinical Indications

**FDA-Approved Indication:**
- Treatment of dry eye disease
- To increase tear production
- In patients whose tear production is suppressed due to ocular inflammation

**Best Candidates:**
- Moderate to severe dry eye
- Evidence of inflammation
- Inadequate response to artificial tears
- Aqueous deficiency component

## Dosing and Administration

**Standard Dosing:**
- One drop in each eye twice daily
- Approximately 12 hours apart
- Allow 15 minutes before inserting contact lenses
- Wait 15 minutes after artificial tears

**Duration of Therapy:**
- Minimum 3-6 months for effect
- Continued use maintains benefit
- Discontinuation may lead to recurrence

## Efficacy

**Clinical Outcomes:**
- Increased tear production
- Reduced symptoms
- Improved ocular surface staining
- Benefit in 4-6 months typically

**Signs of Response:**
- Improved Schirmer test
- Reduced staining
- Better TBUT
- Symptom improvement

## Side Effects

**Common:**
- Burning/stinging upon instillation (up to 17%)
- May decrease with continued use
- Often related to emulsion vehicle

**Less Common:**
- Conjunctival hyperemia
- Eye discharge
- Foreign body sensation
- Blurred vision

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Cyclosporine inhibits T-cell activation
2. Effect takes 3-6 months
3. Dosing is BID
4. Burning is common but often improves
5. Addresses inflammatory component of dry eye
6. Best for moderate to severe dry eye
