# Lifitegrast Ophthalmic Solution (Xiidra)

## Introduction

Lifitegrast ophthalmic solution 5% (Xiidra) is a lymphocyte function-associated antigen-1 (LFA-1) antagonist approved for the treatment of dry eye disease. It represents a different mechanism of action compared to cyclosporine.

## Mechanism of Action

**LFA-1 Antagonism:**
- Binds to LFA-1 on T-cells
- Blocks interaction with ICAM-1
- Prevents T-cell adhesion to epithelial cells
- Reduces inflammatory cascade

**Anti-inflammatory Effects:**
- Decreases cytokine release
- Reduces T-cell-mediated inflammation
- Decreases MMP-9 production

## Clinical Indications

**FDA-Approved Indication:**
- Treatment of signs and symptoms of dry eye disease

**Best Candidates:**
- Moderate dry eye
- Symptoms not controlled by artificial tears
- Patients wanting faster onset than cyclosporine
- Evidence of inflammation

## Dosing and Administration

**Standard Dosing:**
- One drop in each eye twice daily
- Approximately 12 hours apart

**Duration:**
- Effect may be seen in 2-4 weeks
- Faster onset than cyclosporine
- Continued use maintains benefit

## Efficacy

**Clinical Outcomes:**
- Improves symptoms (eye dryness)
- Improves signs (inferior corneal staining)
- Benefit seen in 12 weeks in studies
- Some patients improve faster

## Side Effects

**Common:**
- Dysgeusia (altered taste) - up to 17%
- Visual disturbance
- Conjunctival hyperemia
- Eye irritation

**Less Common:**
- Headache
- Blurred vision

## Comparison to Cyclosporine

| Feature | Lifitegrast | Cyclosporine |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Mechanism | LFA-1 antagonist | Calcineurin inhibitor |
| Onset | 2-4 weeks | 3-6 months |
| Dosing | BID | BID |
| Burning | Less common | Common |
| Taste disturbance | Common | Rare |

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. Lifitegrast is an LFA-1 antagonist
2. Onset is faster than cyclosporine (2-4 weeks)
3. Altered taste is common side effect
4. Dosing is BID
5. Approved for both signs and symptoms
6. Good alternative if cyclosporine not tolerated
