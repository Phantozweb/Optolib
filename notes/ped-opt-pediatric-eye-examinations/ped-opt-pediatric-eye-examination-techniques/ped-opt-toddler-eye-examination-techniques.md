# Toddler Eye Examination Techniques (1-3 years)

## Introduction

Examining toddlers presents unique challenges that require modified techniques, creative approaches, and significant patience. The period from 1-3 years represents a critical time in visual development where detection of strabismus, amblyopia, and refractive errors can prevent lifelong visual impairment.

## Visual Acuity Assessment

### Test Selection by Age and Ability

**12-24 Months:**
- Preferential looking (Teller cards)
- Cardiff cards
- Behavioral observations
- Fixation preference testing

**24-36 Months:**
- Lea symbols
- Allen pictures
- HOTV matching

### Preferential Looking Technique

Children instinctively look at a patterned stimulus rather than a homogeneous gray field. By presenting grating patterns of increasing fineness, grating acuity can be measured.

## Cycloplegic Retinoscopy

**Agent Selection:**
| Age | Agent | Concentration | Notes |
|-----|-------|--------------|-------|
| 12-24 months | Cyclopentolate | 0.5% | May combine with phenylephrine |
| 24-36 months | Cyclopentolate | 0.5-1% | Standard choice |

## Ocular Alignment Assessment

### Cover Testing

**Technique Modifications:**
- Use engaging fixation target
- Cover paddle with picture or toy
- Brief cover periods
- Multiple attempts if needed

### Pseudostrabismus Assessment

**Differentiation:**
| Feature | Pseudostrabismus | True Strabismus |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Cover test | No movement | Movement detected |
| Hirschberg | Symmetric reflexes | Asymmetric reflexes |
| Red reflex | Symmetric | Asymmetric |

## Licensing Exam Points

1. Choosing appropriate acuity test by age
2. Differentiating pseudostrabismus from true esotropia
3. Cycloplegic agent selection
4. Fixation preference interpretation

## Summary

Successful examination of toddlers requires flexibility, creativity, and patience. Early detection of vision problems during this critical period can prevent lifelong visual impairment.
