# DEWS II Diagnostic Algorithm

## Introduction

The TFOS DEWS II diagnostic algorithm provides a systematic, evidence-based approach to diagnosing dry eye disease. This stepwise protocol ensures comprehensive evaluation while maintaining clinical efficiency.

## The Diagnostic Algorithm

### Step 1: Symptom Assessment

The algorithm begins with symptom evaluation using validated questionnaires:

**Recommended Screening Tools:**
- OSDI (Ocular Surface Disease Index)
- SPEED (Standard Patient Evaluation of Eye Dryness)
- DEQ-5 (Dry Eye Questionnaire-5)

**Key Symptoms to Assess:**
- Ocular dryness
- Grittiness or foreign body sensation
- Burning or stinging
- Redness
- Visual fluctuation
- Light sensitivity
- Discomfort worsened by wind, smoke, or dry environments

**Red Flag Symptoms** (suggesting alternative diagnosis):
- Severe pain disproportionate to signs
- Marked discharge
- Rapid vision loss
- Severe photophobia
- Systemic symptoms suggesting autoimmune disease

### Step 2: Risk Factor Assessment

**Systematic History Taking:**

1. **Demographics**
   - Age, Sex, Ethnicity

2. **Ocular History**
   - Previous dry eye diagnosis
   - Contact lens wear
   - Ocular surgery (especially LASIK, PRK)
   - Current eye medications

3. **Systemic Conditions**
   - Autoimmune diseases
   - Diabetes
   - Thyroid disorders
   - Skin conditions (rosacea, psoriasis)

4. **Medications**
   - Antihistamines
   - Antidepressants
   - Anticholinergics
   - Diuretics
   - Beta-blockers
   - Isotretinoin

5. **Environmental Factors**
   - Workplace conditions (air conditioning, heating)
   - Digital device use
   - Outdoor exposure
   - Smoking

### Step 3: Non-Invasive Tests (First)

These tests should be performed before any invasive procedures:

**1. Non-Invasive Tear Break-Up Time (NITBUT)**
- Instrument-based measurement (Placido disc, keratometer)
- Normal: >10 seconds
- Abnormal: <10 seconds

**2. Tear Meniscus Height**
- Measured by OCT or slit lamp
- Normal: ≥0.2 mm
- Reduced values suggest aqueous deficiency

**3. Meibography**
- Infrared imaging of meibomian glands
- Assesses gland morphology and dropout
- Quantifies gland loss percentage

**4. Lipid Layer Assessment**
- Interferometry (Lipiview, DR-1)
- Normal thickness: >60 nm

### Step 4: Invasive Tests

**After non-invasive assessment, perform:**

**1. Tear Osmolarity**
- Point-of-care testing (TearLab)
- Normal: <308 mOsm/L
- Abnormal: >308 mOsm/L or >8 mOsm/L inter-eye difference

**2. MMP-9 Testing (InflammaDry)**
- Detects inflammatory marker
- Positive indicates ocular surface inflammation

**3. Fluorescein Tear Break-Up Time (FTBUT)**
- Apply fluorescein dye
- Measure time from blink to first dry spot
- Normal: ≥10 seconds

**4. Ocular Surface Staining**
- Fluorescein for cornea
- Lissamine green for conjunctiva
- Oxford or NEI grading scales

**5. Schirmer Test**
- With anesthesia (basal secretion)
- Normal: >10 mm in 5 minutes
- Abnormal: ≤5-10 mm in 5 minutes

### Step 5: Meibomian Gland Assessment

**Systematic Evaluation:**

1. **Lid Margin Examination**
   - Vascular crossing
   - Telangiectasia
   - Plugging
   - Notching

2. **Gland Expression**
   - Apply pressure to tarsal plate
   - Assess expressibility (0-3 scale)
   - Evaluate meibum quality

3. **Gland Dysfunction Grading**
   - Mild: 0-1 gland expressing
   - Moderate: 2-3 glands expressing
   - Severe: No expression possible

### Step 6: Classification and Staging

**Based on test results, classify:**

**Aqueous Deficient Dry Eye Indicators:**
- Low Schirmer test
- Reduced tear meniscus height
- High osmolarity
- Interpalpebral staining pattern

**Evaporative Dry Eye Indicators:**
- Low TBUT with normal Schirmer
- Meibomian gland changes
- Abnormal meibum
- Lipid layer deficiency

**Mixed Mechanism:**
- Features of both subtypes
- Most common presentation

## Clinical Pearls for Licensing Exams

1. **Always perform non-invasive tests before invasive tests**
2. **Tear osmolarity is the most sensitive single test**
3. **TBUT < 10 seconds indicates tear film instability**
4. **Schirmer ≤ 5 mm indicates severe aqueous deficiency**
5. **Most patients have mixed mechanism dry eye**
6. **Symptom-sign discrepancy is common**
