# OSDI Questionnaire Administration

## Introduction

The Ocular Surface Disease Index (OSDI) is a validated questionnaire used to assess dry eye symptoms and their impact on vision-related function. It is widely used in clinical practice and research.

## Questionnaire Structure

### Domains (12 Questions Total)

**Ocular Discomfort (3 questions):**
- Light sensitivity
- Grittiness
- Pain/soreness

**Visual Function (6 questions):**
- Reading
- Driving at night
- Computer use
- Watching TV
- Blurred vision

**Environmental Triggers (3 questions):**
- Wind
- Low humidity
- Air conditioning

## Scoring

### Response Options
- 0 = None of the time
- 1 = Some of the time
- 2 = Half of the time
- 3 = Most of the time
- 4 = All of the time

### Calculation

**OSDI Score = (Sum of Scores × 100) / (4 × Number of Questions Answered)**

**Range:** 0-100

### Interpretation

| Score | Severity |
|-------|----------|
| 0-12 | Normal |
| 13-22 | Mild |
| 23-32 | Moderate |
| 33-100 | Severe |

## Clinical Application

### Uses
- Screening for dry eye
- Assessing severity
- Monitoring treatment response
- Research endpoint

### Administration
- Self-administered typically
- 5-10 minutes to complete
- Can be done in waiting room
- Validated in multiple languages

## Limitations

1. Symptom-based, not diagnostic
2. Can be affected by other conditions
3. Some questions may not apply
4. Cultural/language considerations

## Clinical Pearls for Exams

1. OSDI has 12 questions in 3 domains
2. Score ranges from 0-100
3. Score >13 suggests dry eye
4. Used to assess severity and monitor treatment
5. Validated and widely used
6. Has limitations - symptoms don't always correlate with signs
