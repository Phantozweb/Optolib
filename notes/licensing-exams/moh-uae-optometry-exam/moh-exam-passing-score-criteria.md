# MOH Exam Passing Score & Criteria

## Introduction

The Ministry of Health (MOH) United Arab Emirates licensing examination for optometrists represents a critical milestone for healthcare professionals seeking to practice optometry under the federal health authority jurisdiction. This examination encompasses all emirates except Dubai and Abu Dhabi, which maintain their separate licensing authorities (DHA and DOH respectively). Understanding the passing score requirements and evaluation criteria is paramount for successful licensure and career advancement in the UAE healthcare system.

The MOH examination framework is designed to ensure that optometry professionals possess the necessary knowledge, skills, and competencies to deliver safe, effective, and ethical patient care. This comprehensive guide provides detailed information about passing scores, competency thresholds, examination structure, and the complete licensing process.

## 1. Passing Score Requirements

### Minimum Passing Score

The MOH Optometry examination requires candidates to achieve a minimum passing score of **60%** to qualify for licensure consideration. This threshold represents the minimum acceptable level of competency for independent optometric practice in the UAE. The passing standard is established through rigorous psychometric analysis and aligns with international best practices for healthcare professional licensing examinations.

### Score Distribution Across Competency Domains

The examination evaluates candidates across multiple competency domains, each carrying specific weight in the overall score calculation:

| Competency Domain | Weight | Maximum Points |
|-------------------|--------|----------------|
| Clinical Optometry & Refraction | 30% | 30 points |
| Ocular Disease & Pathology | 25% | 25 points |
| Optical Dispensing & Lens Technology | 15% | 15 points |
| Contact Lens Practice | 15% | 15 points |
| Professional Ethics & Regulations | 10% | 10 points |
| Pediatric & Special Populations | 5% | 5 points |

### Section-Specific Minimum Requirements

In addition to the overall passing score, candidates must demonstrate minimum competency in critical clinical domains:

- **Clinical Optometry & Refraction**: Minimum 50% required
- **Ocular Disease & Pathology**: Minimum 50% required  
- **Professional Ethics & Regulations**: Minimum 60% required (elevated threshold due to patient safety implications)

Failure to meet section-specific minimums may result in overall examination failure, even if the total score exceeds 60%.

### Examination Structure and Scoring

**Format Overview:**
- Total Questions: 100 multiple-choice questions (MCQs)
- Time Allocation: 2 hours (120 minutes)
- Question Type: Single best answer format
- Scoring Method: Equal weight per question (1 point each)
- Negative Marking: None applied

**Question Categories:**
- Knowledge-based questions: 40%
- Clinical application questions: 40%
- Case-based reasoning questions: 20%

### Score Validity Period

A passing MOH examination score remains valid for **two (2) years** from the examination date. During this period, candidates must complete all licensing requirements including primary source verification, document submission, and license activation. Failure to complete licensing within the validity period necessitates examination retake.

## 2. Score Calculation Methodology

### Raw Score Computation

The raw score is calculated using a straightforward formula:

**Raw Score = (Number of Correct Answers / Total Questions) × 100**

For example, if a candidate correctly answers 68 questions out of 100:
- Raw Score = (68/100) × 100 = 68%

### Domain-Specific Score Calculation

Each competency domain is scored independently:

1. **Clinical Optometry & Refraction (30 questions)**
   - Covers: Subjective refraction, objective refraction, binocular vision assessment, accommodation testing
   - Sub-domain weighting: Refraction (60%), Binocular vision (25%), Accommodation (15%)

2. **Ocular Disease & Pathology (25 questions)**
   - Covers: Anterior segment disorders, posterior segment pathology, systemic disease manifestations
   - Emphasis on: Diagnosis (50%), Management planning (35%), Referral criteria (15%)

3. **Optical Dispensing & Lens Technology (15 questions)**
   - Covers: Lens design, prism calculations, frame selection, lens materials
   - Focus on: Practical application and patient education

4. **Contact Lens Practice (15 questions)**
   - Covers: Soft lens fitting, rigid lens fitting, specialty lenses, complications management
   - Includes: Patient selection, fitting procedures, follow-up care

5. **Professional Ethics & Regulations (10 questions)**
   - Covers: MOH regulations, patient rights, professional conduct, documentation requirements
   - Case-based scenarios: 60% of questions in this domain

6. **Pediatric & Special Populations (5 questions)**
   - Covers: Pediatric assessment, geriatric considerations, patients with disabilities

### Weighted Score Calculation

The weighted score incorporates domain importance:

**Weighted Score = Σ (Domain Score × Domain Weight)**

This approach ensures that performance in high-importance domains appropriately influences the final score.

### Score Rounding Protocol

- Scores are rounded to the nearest whole number
- Scores of 59.5% and above round up to 60% (passing)
- Scores below 59.5% round down to 59% (failing)

### Official Score Report Components

Candidates receive a comprehensive score report including:

- Overall percentage score
- Domain-by-domain performance breakdown
- Comparison to passing standards
- Percentile ranking among examination cohort
- Diagnostic feedback on areas requiring improvement
- Recommendations for professional development

## 3. Competency Thresholds

### Defining Competency Standards

Competency thresholds represent the minimum acceptable level of knowledge, skill, and professional judgment required for safe optometric practice. These thresholds are established through collaboration between MOH health regulation authorities, subject matter experts, and international advisory panels to ensure alignment with global standards.

### Competency Level Classifications

| Competency Level | Score Range | Professional Interpretation |
|------------------|-------------|----------------------------|
| Expert | 90-100% | Exceptional mastery, suitable for advanced clinical roles and mentorship |
| Advanced | 75-89% | Strong knowledge base, ready for independent practice with minimal supervision |
| Proficient | 60-74% | Meets minimum standards, ready for supervised practice with gradual independence |
| Below Standard | Below 60% | Does not meet minimum competency requirements |

### Clinical Competency Requirements

**Diagnostic Competencies:**
- Ability to perform comprehensive eye examinations
- Competence in detecting common ocular abnormalities
- Understanding of appropriate diagnostic testing indications
- Skill in interpreting examination findings accurately

**Management Competencies:**
- Development of appropriate treatment plans
- Understanding of optical correction options
- Knowledge of contact lens management
- Recognition of conditions requiring specialist referral

**Communication Competencies:**
- Patient education skills
- Informed consent procedures
- Professional documentation
- Interprofessional communication

### Borderline Performance Review

Candidates scoring between 58-62% undergo additional review:

- Manual verification of examination responses
- Detailed section analysis
- Committee review of borderline cases
- Possible recommendation for supplementary clinical assessment

### Competency Certification

Successful candidates receive documentation indicating:
- Overall competency level achieved
- Domain-specific strengths identified
- Areas recommended for continued professional development
- Eligibility for specific practice scopes

## 4. Examination Failure Procedures

### Immediate Post-Failure Actions

Candidates who do not achieve the passing score will:

1. Receive detailed score report within 14 business days
2. Access diagnostic feedback identifying specific weak areas
3. Receive comparative performance data against successful candidates
4. Obtain study recommendations based on performance gaps

### Score Review Request Process

Candidates may request score verification within **30 calendar days** of receiving results:

| Review Component | Details |
|-----------------|---------|
| Review Fee | AED 250 (non-refundable) |
| Process | Administrative verification of answer key and score calculation |
| Timeline | Results within 10 business days |
| Scope | Verification of administrative accuracy, not re-grading of responses |

### Formal Appeal Process

For candidates with extenuating circumstances:

1. **Written Appeal Submission**
   - Timeline: Within 30 days of results
   - Required: Detailed written statement
   - Documentation: Supporting evidence (medical certificates, emergency documentation)

2. **Appeal Review Fee**
   - Fee: AED 500
   - Refund: Full refund if appeal successful

3. **Appeal Decision Timeline**
   - Review period: 30 business days
   - Written decision provided to candidate

### Failure Classification and Recommendations

**Marginal Failure (55-59%):**
- Recommended action: Focused study on identified weak areas
- Suggested resources: Practice examinations, targeted review courses
- Minimum waiting period: 30 days

**Moderate Failure (45-54%):**
- Recommended action: Comprehensive review course enrollment
- Suggested resources: Structured study program, mentor guidance
- Minimum waiting period: 60 days

**Significant Failure (Below 45%):**
- Recommended action: Complete educational program review
- Suggested resources: Academic consultation, supervised practice hours
- Minimum waiting period: 90 days

### Support Resources for Failed Candidates

MOH provides access to:
- Official preparation courses through accredited providers
- Online study resource library
- Practice examination portals
- Professional mentor matching programs
- Study group facilitation

## 5. Re-examination Policies

### Tiered Waiting Period Structure

The MOH implements progressively longer waiting periods based on attempt number:

| Attempt Number | Waiting Period | Additional Requirements |
|----------------|----------------|------------------------|
| 1st Retake | 30 days | Recommended self-study |
| 2nd Retake | 60 days | Recommended preparation course |
| 3rd Retake | 90 days | Mandatory preparation course completion certificate |
| 4th Retake | 6 months | Full re-application and educational review |

### Maximum Attempt Limitations

- **Total Attempts Permitted**: 4 examination attempts within a 3-year period
- **After 4 Failed Attempts**: Candidates must:
  - Wait minimum 1 year before reapplying
  - Complete additional educational requirements as determined by MOH
  - Provide evidence of substantial professional development activities
  - Obtain recommendation from recognized educational institution

### Re-examination Fee Structure

| Attempt | Examination Fee (AED) | Application Processing (AED) |
|---------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| Initial | 1,000 | 500 |
| 1st Retake | 1,000 | 0 |
| 2nd Retake | 1,200 | 0 |
| 3rd Retake | 1,500 | 0 |
| 4th Retake | 2,000 | 500 (full re-application) |

### Re-examination Application Process

1. **Online Portal Application**
   - Platform: MOH licensing portal
   - Required: Updated credentials if applicable

2. **Document Submission**
   - Updated CV if professional experience changed
   - Continuing education certificates completed

3. **Fee Payment**
   - Payment methods: Credit card, bank transfer
   - Confirmation receipt required for scheduling

4. **Examination Scheduling**
   - Available dates provided based on testing center capacity
   - Confirmation email with examination details

### Special Circumstances

**Technical Difficulties During Examination:**
- Complimentary re-examination offered
- No waiting period required
- Priority scheduling provided

**Medical Emergency on Examination Day:**
- Rescheduling without penalty
- Required: Valid medical documentation
- Fee deferred to new examination date

**Bereavement or Family Emergency:**
- Case-by-case consideration
- Documentation required
- Flexible rescheduling

## 6. License Issuance Process

### Post-Examination Requirements

After achieving a passing examination score, candidates must complete several mandatory steps:

**1. Primary Source Verification (PSV)**
- Purpose: Authentication of all professional credentials
- Duration: 2-8 weeks depending on document origin
- Required verifications:
  - Educational degree/diploma from issuing institution
  - Professional license(s) from all previous licensing authorities
  - Employment experience letters from employers
  - Professional registration certificates

**2. Good Standing Certificate**
- Required from: All jurisdictions where previously licensed
- Validity: Must be issued within 3 months of application
- Content: Confirmation of no disciplinary actions or restrictions
- Authentication: Some jurisdictions require embassy attestation

**3. Background Verification**
- Criminal background check from country of residence
- Professional misconduct screening
- Verification against healthcare sanctions lists

### Required Documentation for License Issuance

| Document | Specifications |
|----------|----------------|
| Passport Copy | Valid minimum 6 months, clear color copy |
| Passport Photograph | White background, recent (within 6 months), professional attire |
| Educational Certificates | Original or attested copies, translated if not in English/Arabic |
| Transcripts | Complete academic record, officially sealed |
| Experience Certificates | Minimum 2 years post-qualification, on official letterhead |
| MOH Examination Result | Passing score certificate |
| Good Standing Certificate(s) | From all previous licensing jurisdictions |
| Medical Fitness Certificate | From UAE-approved medical facility |
| Professional Indemnity Insurance | Minimum coverage AED 500,000 per incident |
| Language Proficiency | IELTS 6.0 or equivalent (if applicable) |

### License Processing Timeline

| Processing Stage | Duration |
|-----------------|----------|
| Initial Document Review | 3-5 business days |
| Primary Source Verification | 2-8 weeks |
| Background Check Processing | 1-2 weeks |
| Final License Committee Review | 5-10 business days |
| License Certificate Issuance | 3-5 business days |

**Total Typical Timeline: 4-10 weeks from complete application submission**

### License Categories and Classifications

**Full Practice License:**
- Complete optometry practice privileges
- All approved optometric procedures
- Independent practice authority
- Valid for 1 year, renewable annually

**Supervised Practice License:**
- Practice under senior optometrist supervision
- Limited procedure authorization
- Duration: 6 months to 1 year
- Conversion to full license upon satisfactory performance evaluation

**Temporary License:**
- Short-term authorization for specific projects
- Maximum duration: 3 months
- Non-renewable, requires new application for extension

### License Fee Structure

| License Type | Fee (AED) | Validity Period |
|--------------|-----------|-----------------|
| New License | 3,000 | 1 year |
| Annual Renewal | 2,500 | 1 year |
| License Amendment | 500 | Per change |
| License Replacement | 200 | One-time |
| Good Standing Certificate | 150 | Per certificate |

## 7. License Activation Requirements

### Employer Registration Process

Upon license issuance, activation requires employer sponsorship:

1. **Employment Contract Verification**
   - Contract with MOH-licensed healthcare facility
   - Facility must hold valid MOH facility license
   - Position must align with license scope

2. **Facility Sponsorship**
   - Facility submits sponsorship request through MOH portal
   - Professional license linked to facility license
   - Practice location officially designated

3. **Position Designation**
   - Job title aligned with license category
   - Scope of practice defined by facility and license
   - Supervision structure established if applicable

### Professional Insurance Requirements

- **Minimum Coverage**: AED 500,000 per incident
- **Annual Aggregate**: AED 1,000,000 minimum
- **Provider**: UAE-approved insurance company
- **Certificate**: Must name MOH as certificate holder

### Practice Permit Issuance

Upon successful activation, optometrists receive:
- Practice permit card
- Professional identification badge
- MOH portal access credentials
- Facility association confirmation

### Activation Timeline Summary

| Activation Step | Processing Time |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Employer Registration | 1-3 business days |
| Insurance Verification | 2-5 business days |
| Facility Association | 1-2 business days |
| Permit Generation | Same day after approval |

## 8. Scope of Practice Authorization

### Permitted Clinical Procedures

MOH-licensed optometrists are authorized to perform:

**Comprehensive Eye Examinations:**
- Visual acuity assessment (distance and near)
- Refraction (subjective and objective)
- Binocular vision evaluation
- Accommodation testing
- Color vision assessment
- Contrast sensitivity testing
- Visual field screening and analysis

**Ocular Health Assessment:**
- External eye examination
- Anterior segment biomicroscopy
- Intraocular pressure measurement
- Dilated fundus examination
- Retinal evaluation
- Optical coherence tomography (OCT)
- Corneal topography and tomography

**Contact Lens Services:**
- Soft contact lens fitting and dispensing
- Rigid gas permeable lens fitting
- Specialty lens fitting (toric, multifocal, cosmetic)
- Therapeutic bandage contact lens application
- Contact lens aftercare and management

**Vision Rehabilitation:**
- Low vision assessment
- Low vision device prescription
- Vision therapy program development
- Pediatric vision assessment

### Prescriptive Authority

Within the MOH optometry scope:

- Spectacle prescriptions (single vision, bifocal, progressive)
- Contact lens prescriptions
- Non-prescription ophthalmic products
- Therapeutic pharmaceutical agents (as per specific MOH TPA certification)
- Diagnostic pharmaceutical agents for examination purposes

### Practice Limitations

MOH optometrists must NOT perform:

1. Surgical procedures of any kind
2. Injectable medication administration
3. Oral medication prescription
4. Laser procedures
5. Conditions requiring surgical intervention management
6. Treatment beyond approved scope of practice

### Mandatory Referral Requirements

Conditions requiring ophthalmologist referral:

- Glaucoma (suspected or confirmed)
- Cataract affecting visual function
- Retinal pathology (diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration)
- Uveitis and intraocular inflammation
- Optic nerve abnormalities
- Ocular tumors
- Neuro-ophthalmic conditions
- Pediatric conditions requiring surgical intervention
- Corneal pathology beyond superficial treatment

## 9. Continuing Professional Development Requirements

### Annual CPD Requirements

| CPD Category | Minimum Hours | Maximum Claimable |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Clinical Optometry | 12 | No limit |
| Ocular Disease | 8 | No limit |
| Professional Ethics | 4 | No limit |
| Practice Management | 3 | No limit |
| Technology & Innovation | 3 | No limit |
| **Total Annual Requirement** | **30 hours** | - |

### Approved CPD Activities

**Category 1: Formal Education Activities**
- International conferences and congresses
- National symposia and seminars
- Accredited workshops
- University continuing education courses
- Online accredited programs

**Category 2: Professional Development Activities**
- Journal club participation
- Case presentation at professional meetings
- Peer review activities
- Research project involvement
- Publication in peer-reviewed journals

**Category 3: Self-Directed Learning**
- Online learning modules with assessment
- Professional journal reading with verification
- Educational webinar participation
- Podcast learning with completion verification

### Approved CPD Providers

MOH recognizes continuing education from:
- MOH-accredited programs
- Ministry of Health and Prevention (MOHAP) programs
- International optometry associations (AOA, WCO, ICO)
- Recognized optometry schools worldwide
- Accredited online education platforms

### CPD Documentation Requirements

Required for CPD credit claim:
- Certificate of completion with date
- Activity description and learning objectives
- Contact hours claimed
- Provider accreditation details
- Proof of attendance or completion

### Non-Compliance Consequences

**First Year Non-Compliance:**
- Formal warning letter
- Mandatory remediation plan development
- Additional 15 CPD hours required

**Second Year Non-Compliance:**
- License suspension (3 months)
- Mandatory CPD completion
- Reinstatement fee: AED 1,500

**Third Year Non-Compliance:**
- License revocation
- Mandatory 1-year waiting period
- Re-application with full documentation

## 10. Professional Regulations and Standards

### MOH Regulatory Framework

Professional practice is governed by:
- MOH Health Regulation Department
- Federal healthcare laws and regulations
- Professional code of conduct
- Scope of practice regulations

### Professional Standards Requirements

**Standard of Care:**
- Evidence-based practice implementation
- Current knowledge maintenance
- Appropriate equipment utilization
- Comprehensive documentation

**Patient Rights:**
- Informed consent for all procedures
- Privacy and confidentiality protection
- Medical record access facilitation
- Second opinion facilitation
- Complaint resolution process

**Professional Conduct:**
- Integrity and honesty in all interactions
- Conflict of interest avoidance
- Appropriate professional advertising
- Professional boundary maintenance
- Collegial respect and cooperation

### Record Keeping Standards

**Required Documentation:**
- Complete patient demographic information
- Chief complaint and detailed history
- Comprehensive examination findings
- Diagnosis and clinical assessment
- Treatment plan with rationale
- All prescriptions issued
- Referral documentation
- Follow-up instructions and schedule

**Retention Requirements:**
- Adult patient records: 10 years minimum
- Pediatric records: Until age 25 or 10 years (whichever longer)
- Records with adverse outcomes: 25 years
- Electronic records: Same retention as paper records

### Disciplinary Framework

**Categories of Professional Misconduct:**
1. Practicing without valid license
2. Practicing beyond authorized scope
3. Patient abandonment
4. Confidentiality breach
5. False or misleading advertising
6. Substance abuse affecting practice
7. Sexual misconduct or harassment
8. Fraud or misrepresentation
9. Professional negligence
10. Inappropriate referral practices

**Progressive Disciplinary Actions:**
1. Written warning with required remediation
2. Mandatory professional training
3. Supervised practice requirement
4. License suspension (temporary)
5. License revocation (permanent)
