# MOH Exam Mock Questions & Practice

## Introduction

The Ministry of Health (MOH) UAE Optometry licensing examination is a comprehensive assessment designed to evaluate the competency of optometrists seeking to practice in the United Arab Emirates under the MOH jurisdiction. This practice examination contains over 50 multiple-choice questions covering all major domains of optometric practice, including clinical optometry, ocular disease and pathology, optical dispensing, contact lenses, pediatric optometry, neuro-ophthalmology, pharmacology, and professional regulations.

These mock questions are carefully crafted to mirror the style, difficulty level, and content distribution of the actual MOH examination. Each question includes a detailed explanation of the correct answer, helping candidates understand the underlying concepts and clinical reasoning required for successful examination performance.

## MOH Examination Structure Overview

The MOH Optometry examination typically consists of 100 multiple-choice questions distributed across the following domains:

| Domain | Weight | Question Count |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Clinical Optometry & Refraction | 25% | 25 questions |

## Section 1: Clinical Optometry & Refraction

### Question 1

A 48-year-old patient presents with difficulty reading small print. Distance visual acuity is 20/20 OU. Near visual acuity is J5 with the patient's current glasses. Which of the following is the MOST appropriate initial test?

A) Dynamic retinoscopy
B) Near point of convergence
C) Amplitude of accommodation
D) Cover test at near

**Correct Answer: C) Amplitude of accommodation**

**Explanation:** The patient's age (48 years) and symptoms are classic for presbyopia. The amplitude of accommodation naturally decreases with age, typically reaching approximately 2.50D by age 48. Measuring the amplitude of accommodation (using the push-up method or minus lens method) is the most direct way to assess accommodative function and confirm the diagnosis of presbyopia. While the other tests may provide useful information, amplitude of accommodation is the most specific to the presenting complaint.

### Question 2

During subjective refraction using Jackson Cross Cylinder (JCC), a patient with -2.00 -1.00 x 180 reports that the first position is clearer. The cylinder axis is currently at 180°. Which adjustment should be made?

A) Rotate axis toward 180°
B) Rotate axis toward 90°
C) Rotate axis toward 135°
D) Rotate axis toward 45°

**Correct Answer: A) Rotate axis toward 180°**

**Explanation:** In Jackson Cross Cylinder testing, when the patient reports that the first position (with the minus cylinder axis aligned with the trial frame axis) is clearer, this indicates that the current axis is correct or should be moved toward the direction of the minus cylinder axis. The "pound sign" rule applies: if the first position is clearer, the axis is already close to correct.

### Question 3

A patient's retinoscopy findings show a reflex that moves "with" the sweep in all meridians. The current lens in place is +2.00 DS. What should be done?

A) Add minus power
B) Add plus power
C) The endpoint has been reached
D) Rotate the streak orientation

**Correct Answer: B) Add plus power**

**Explanation:** In retinoscopy, a "with" movement indicates that the far point is behind the eye, meaning the eye is relatively hyperopic compared to the current lens. To neutralize the reflex, plus power must be added. When the reflex shows "with" movement, you need to add plus lenses until the reflex fills the pupil and shows no movement (neutralization).

### Question 4

Which of the following represents the correct approximate magnification of a direct ophthalmoscope in an emmetropic eye?

A) 5×
B) 10×
C) 15×
D) 20×

**Correct Answer: C) 15×**

**Explanation:** The direct ophthalmoscope produces an upright, virtual image that is magnified approximately 15 times in an emmetropic eye. The high magnification makes the direct ophthalmoscope excellent for examining small retinal details, but the small field of view (approximately 10°) limits its use for peripheral examination.

### Question 5

A patient has a distance PD of 62mm. What is the approximate near PD for a working distance of 40cm?

A) 56mm
B) 58mm
C) 60mm
D) 62mm

**Correct Answer: B) 58mm**

**Explanation:** The near pupillary distance (PD) is always smaller than the distance PD due to convergence when viewing near targets. The typical difference between distance and near PD is approximately 3-4mm for a standard working distance of 40cm. For practical purposes, the near PD at 40cm is typically 3-4mm less than the distance PD. Given the distance PD of 62mm, the near PD would be approximately 58mm.

### Question 6

In binocular cross-cylinder testing, if the patient reports the line pairs are equal but the lines appear in front of the target, what is the diagnosis?

A) Emmetropia
B) Hyperopia
C) Myopia
D) Astigmatism

**Correct Answer: B) Hyperopia**

**Explanation:** The binocular cross-cylinder (BCC) test assesses the accommodative response relative to the focal plane of the target. When both line pairs are equal but the lines appear in front of the target plane, this indicates that the patient is accommodating more than required to focus at the target distance. This "lead of accommodation" is characteristic of hyperopia, where the patient must maintain accommodation even for distance vision.

### Question 7

What is the expected minimum amplitude of accommodation for a 30-year-old patient according to Hofstetter's formula?

A) 5.50D
B) 7.50D
C) 9.50D
D) 11.50D

**Correct Answer: B) 7.50D**

**Explanation:** Hofstetter's formula for the expected minimum amplitude of accommodation is: 15 - 0.25 × Age. For a 30-year-old: 15 - 0.25 × 30 = 15 - 7.5 = 7.50D. This is the minimum expected amplitude that should be present in a normal, healthy individual of that age.

### Question 8

Which portion of the retina corresponds to the blind spot on visual field testing?

A) Fovea
B) Optic disc
C) Macula
D) Ora serrata

**Correct Answer: B) Optic disc**

**Explanation:** The blind spot in visual field testing corresponds to the location of the optic disc, which is approximately 15° temporal to the fovea and slightly below the horizontal meridian. The optic disc has no photoreceptors because it is the point where the optic nerve exits the eye and retinal vessels enter, creating a physiological scotoma.

### Question 9

During retinoscopy, a "scissors" reflex is observed. This finding is MOST commonly associated with which condition?

A) Keratoconus
B) Cataract
C) Astigmatism
D) Myopia

**Correct Answer: A) Keratoconus**

**Explanation:** A "scissors" reflex during retinoscopy, where the reflex appears to split into two bands moving in opposite directions, is most commonly associated with keratoconus or other corneal irregularities. This occurs due to the irregular astigmatism and steepening of the cornea that characterizes keratoconus.

### Question 10

Which of the following is the correct transposition of the prescription: -3.00 -1.50 x 90?

A) -4.50 +1.50 x 180
B) -4.50 +1.50 x 90
C) -3.00 +1.50 x 180
D) -1.50 +1.50 x 180

**Correct Answer: A) -4.50 +1.50 x 180**

**Explanation:** To transpose from minus cylinder form to plus cylinder form: (1) Add the sphere and cylinder powers algebraically: -3.00 + (-1.50) = -4.50. (2) Change the sign of the cylinder: -1.50 becomes +1.50. (3) Rotate the axis by 90°: 90° becomes 180°. The transposed prescription is -4.50 +1.50 x 180.

## Section 2: Ocular Disease & Pathology

### Question 11

A 65-year-old diabetic patient presents with sudden vision loss in the right eye. Fundus examination shows a cherry-red spot with surrounding pale retina. What is the MOST likely diagnosis?

A) Central retinal vein occlusion (CRVO)
B) Central retinal artery occlusion (CRAO)
C) Retinal detachment
D) Vitreous hemorrhage

**Correct Answer: B) Central retinal artery occlusion (CRAO)**

**Explanation:** The classic presentation of central retinal artery occlusion (CRAO) includes sudden, painless vision loss with a cherry-red spot at the fovea surrounded by pale, ischemic retina. The cherry-red spot appears because the fovea receives its blood supply from the choroid, while the surrounding retina becomes pale due to lack of perfusion. CRAO is an ophthalmic emergency requiring immediate intervention.

### Question 12

Which of the following is NOT a characteristic feature of primary open-angle glaucoma (POAG)?

A) Elevated intraocular pressure
B) Open anterior chamber angle
C) Optic nerve cupping
D) Pigment dispersion on the corneal endothelium

**Correct Answer: D) Pigment dispersion on the corneal endothelium**

**Explanation:** Pigment dispersion on the corneal endothelium (Krukenberg spindle) is characteristic of pigment dispersion syndrome, not primary open-angle glaucoma. POAG is characterized by elevated intraocular pressure, an open anterior chamber angle on gonioscopy, and progressive optic nerve cupping with corresponding visual field loss.

### Question 13

A patient presents with a painful red eye, photophobia, and small, non-reactive pupil. Slit lamp examination shows cells and flare in the anterior chamber. What is the MOST appropriate initial treatment?

A) Topical antibiotics
B) Topical corticosteroids
C) Topical beta-blockers
D) Oral antivirals

**Correct Answer: B) Topical corticosteroids**

**Explanation:** The clinical presentation is consistent with anterior uveitis (iritis), characterized by pain, photophobia, small irregular pupil, and cells and flare in the anterior chamber. The mainstay of treatment for anterior uveitis is topical corticosteroids to reduce inflammation, along with cycloplegic agents to relieve ciliary spasm and prevent posterior synechiae formation.

### Question 14

In the classification of diabetic retinopathy, which finding indicates "severe non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy" according to ETDRS criteria?

A) Presence of cotton-wool spots
B) Intraretinal microvascular abnormalities (IRMA)
C) Venous beading in two quadrants
D) Any of the "4-2-1 rule" features

**Correct Answer: D) Any of the "4-2-1 rule" features**

**Explanation:** According to the Early Treatment Diabetic Retinopathy Study (ETDRS), severe non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy (NPDR) is defined by the presence of any one of the "4-2-1 rule" features: (1) Severe retinal hemorrhages in all 4 quadrants, (2) Venous beading in 2 or more quadrants, or (3) Intraretinal microvascular abnormalities (IRMA) in 1 or more quadrants.

### Question 15

Which type of cataract is MOST commonly associated with chronic corticosteroid use?

A) Nuclear sclerotic cataract
B) Cortical cataract
C) Posterior subcapsular cataract
D) Anterior subcapsular cataract

**Correct Answer: C) Posterior subcapsular cataract**

**Explanation:** Posterior subcapsular cataract (PSC) is the type most commonly associated with chronic corticosteroid use, whether systemic, inhaled, or topical. PSCs appear as granular opacities at the posterior capsule and cause significant visual symptoms, particularly glare and difficulty with near vision.

### Question 16

A patient presents with a triangular-shaped area of conjunctival growth extending onto the cornea from the nasal side. What is the diagnosis?

A) Pinguecula
B) Pterygium
C) Conjunctival nevus
D) Squamous cell carcinoma

**Correct Answer: B) Pterygium**

**Explanation:** A pterygium is a triangular, fibrovascular growth of conjunctival tissue that extends onto the cornea, typically from the nasal side due to increased UV exposure. The pterygium consists of a head (the advancing edge on the cornea), a neck, and a body. It is associated with UV exposure, chronic irritation, and dry conditions.

### Question 17

In age-related macular degeneration (AMD), which finding is characteristic of the "wet" form?

A) Drusen
B) Geographic atrophy
C) Choroidal neovascularization
D) Pigmentary changes

**Correct Answer: C) Choroidal neovascularization**

**Explanation:** Choroidal neovascularization (CNV) is the hallmark of wet (neovascular) age-related macular degeneration. CNV involves the growth of abnormal blood vessels from the choroid through Bruch's membrane into the sub-retinal space. These vessels are fragile and can leak fluid and blood, leading to rapid vision loss.

Which of the following is the most common cause of infectious keratitis in contact lens wearers?

B) Pseudomonas aeruginosa
C) Streptococcus pneumoniae
D) Herpes simplex virus

**Correct Answer: B) Pseudomonas aeruginosa**

**Explanation:** Pseudomonas aeruginosa is the most common cause of bacterial keratitis in contact lens wearers. It is a gram-negative organism that can rapidly progress and cause severe corneal damage. Contact lens wear disrupts the corneal epithelium and creates an environment conducive to Pseudomonas infection.

### Question 19

A patient presents with acute angle-closure glaucoma. Which of the following is the FIRST line of treatment?

A) Topical beta-blocker
B) Oral acetazolamide
C) Laser peripheral iridotomy
D) All of the above simultaneously

**Correct Answer: D) All of the above simultaneously**

**Explanation:** Acute angle-closure glaucoma is an ophthalmic emergency requiring immediate treatment to lower intraocular pressure. The initial management typically includes multiple agents simultaneously: topical beta-blocker, topical alpha-2 agonist, oral or IV carbonic anhydrase inhibitor, and pilocarpine. Laser peripheral iridotomy is the definitive treatment but may be delayed until the cornea clears.

### Question 20

Which layer of the cornea is responsible for the majority of its structural integrity?

A) Epithelium
B) Bowman's layer
C) Stroma
D) Endothelium

**Correct Answer: C) Stroma**

**Explanation:** The corneal stroma comprises approximately 90% of the corneal thickness and provides the majority of its structural integrity. It consists of regularly arranged collagen lamellae embedded in a ground substance. The precise arrangement of collagen fibers is responsible for corneal transparency.

## Section 3: Optical Dispensing & Instrumentation

### Question 21

What is the induced prism in a lens with a power of +4.00 DS when the patient's pupil is located 5mm below the optical center? Use Prentice's rule.

A) 1.00 prism diopter base up
B) 2.00 prism diopters base up
C) 1.00 prism diopter base down
D) 2.00 prism diopters base down

**Correct Answer: B) 2.00 prism diopters base up**

**Explanation:** Prentice's rule states: Prism = F × d, where F is the lens power in diopters and d is the distance from the optical center in centimeters. Prism = 4.00D × 0.5cm = 2.00 prism diopters. For a plus lens, the base is toward the optical center. If the pupil is below the optical center, the base of the induced prism is up.

### Question 22

Which lens material has the HIGHEST Abbé number (lowest chromatic aberration)?

A) Polycarbonate (n=1.59)
B) Trivex (n=1.53)
C) CR-39 (n=1.50)
D) High-index 1.67

**Correct Answer: C) CR-39 (n=1.50)**

**Explanation:** CR-39 has an Abbé number of approximately 58, which is the highest among common ophthalmic lens materials. The Abbé number indicates the material's resistance to chromatic aberration; higher numbers mean less dispersion and better optical quality.

### Question 23

In progressive addition lenses (PALs), what is the term for the area on each side of the corridor that contains unwanted cylinder and distortion?

A) Fitting cross
B) Near zone
C) Distance zone
D) Blend zone

**Correct Answer: D) Blend zone**

**Explanation:** The blend zone (also called the peripheral distortion zone) is the area on either side of the progressive corridor that contains unwanted cylinder and distortion. This is an unavoidable consequence of the progressive design, where the lens must transition from distance to near powers while maintaining surface continuity.

### Question 24

A patient has a vertical imbalance of 3 prism diopters between the two eyes at the reading level. Which solution is MOST appropriate?

A) Adjust the frame fitting
B) Use slab-off prism
C) Change the lens material
D) Increase the add power

**Correct Answer: B) Use slab-off prism**

**Explanation:** When vertical imbalance exceeds approximately 1.5-2.0 prism diopters at near, patients may experience symptoms. Slab-off prism (bicentric grinding) is a technique used to correct vertical imbalance by removing prism from the lower portion of one lens, applied to the lens with the most minus (or least plus) power in the vertical meridian.

### Question 25

What is the effective power of a -6.00 DS spectacle lens when moved from a vertex distance of 12mm to 10mm?

A) -5.88 DS
B) -6.12 DS
C) -5.75 DS
D) -6.25 DS

**Correct Answer: A) -5.88 DS**

**Explanation:** When a minus lens is moved closer to the eye, its effective power decreases (becomes less minus). The formula is: F2 = F1 / (1 - dF1), where d is the change in vertex distance in meters. For moving from 12mm to 10mm (2mm = 0.002m closer): F2 = -6.00 / (1 - 0.002 × -6.00) = -6.00 / 1.012 = -5.93D (approximately -5.88 to -6.00D).

## Section 4: Contact Lens Practice

### Question 26

What is the oxygen transmissibility (Dk/t) threshold generally considered adequate for daily wear soft contact lenses?

A) 10 × 10⁻¹¹
B) 24 × 10⁻¹¹
C) 50 × 10⁻¹¹
D) 100 × 10⁻¹¹

**Correct Answer: B) 24 × 10⁻¹¹**

**Explanation:** For daily wear soft contact lenses, a Dk/t of approximately 24 × 10⁻¹¹ is generally considered adequate to prevent corneal hypoxia during waking hours. This threshold ensures sufficient oxygen reaches the cornea to maintain normal metabolism and prevent complications.

### Question 27

A patient wearing soft contact lenses develops a painful red eye with a white infiltrate in the peripheral cornea. What is the MOST likely diagnosis?

A) Bacterial keratitis
B) Contact lens peripheral ulcer (CLPU)
C) Viral keratitis
D) Fungal keratitis

**Correct Answer: B) Contact lens peripheral ulcer (CLPU)**

**Explanation:** Contact lens peripheral ulcer (CLPU) is a sterile inflammatory condition characterized by a small, well-circumscribed white infiltrate in the peripheral cornea, typically with an overlying epithelial defect. It is thought to be a hypersensitivity reaction to bacterial antigens.

### Question 28

In rigid gas permeable (RGP) lens fitting, what does a "three-point touch" pattern indicate?

A) Flat fitting
B) Steep fitting
C) Optimal fitting
D) Warped cornea

**Correct Answer: C) Optimal fitting**

**Explanation:** A "three-point touch" pattern on fluorescein evaluation indicates an optimal RGP lens fit. This pattern shows light touch at three points: the central cornea and two points on the peripheral cornea. The lens distributes its weight evenly, ensuring good centration, minimal movement, and patient comfort.

### Question 29

A patient with keratoconus is being fitted with a specialty contact lens. Which type of lens design is MOST appropriate for moderate to advanced keratoconus?

A) Spherical soft lens
B) Toric soft lens
C) Scleral lens
D) Standard RGP lens

**Correct Answer: C) Scleral lens**

**Explanation:** For moderate to advanced keratoconus, scleral lenses are often the most appropriate choice. They vault entirely over the cornea and land on the sclera, providing a smooth optical surface that masks the irregular corneal astigmatism while offering excellent comfort and stability.

### Question 30

What is the approximate contact lens power needed for a -5.50 DS spectacle prescription with a 12mm vertex distance?

A) -5.00 DS
B) -5.25 DS
C) -5.50 DS
D) -5.75 DS

**Correct Answer: B) -5.25 DS**

**Explanation:** For myopic corrections, the contact lens power is less minus than the spectacle power. Using the vertex compensation formula: Fc = Fs / (1 - dFs), where d = 0.012m: Fc = -5.50 / (1 - 0.012 × -5.50) = -5.50 / 1.066 = -5.16D, rounded to -5.25D or -5.00D.

Which of the following is a contraindication for soft contact lens wear?

Astigmatism can be corrected with toric soft lenses. Presbyopia can be addressed with multifocal soft lenses or monovision.

What is the recommended maximum wearing time for daily wear soft contact lenses?

## Section 5: Pediatric Optometry & Binocular Vision

### Question 33

A 6-month-old infant is found to have a constant esotropia of 40 prism diopters. What is the MOST appropriate management?

A) Observation until age 2
B) Prescribe the full hyperopic correction
C) Prescribe half the hyperopic correction
D) Immediate surgical referral

**Correct Answer: D) Immediate surgical referral**

**Explanation:** This infant presents with infantile esotropia (congenital esotropia), characterized by large-angle, constant esotropia presenting before 6 months. Unlike accommodative esotropia, this deviation is not significantly reduced by hyperopic correction. Early surgery (ideally before 12-18 months) is recommended to promote binocular vision development.

### Question 34

In the cover-uncover test, if the uncovered eye moves outward (temporally) when the cover is placed over the fellow eye, what type of deviation is present?

A) Esophoria
B) Exophoria
C) Esotropia
D) Exotropia

**Correct Answer: C) Esotropia**

**Explanation:** In the cover-uncover test, a tropia is detected by observing the movement of the uncovered eye when the fellow eye is covered. If the uncovered eye moves outward (temporally) to take up fixation, this indicates that the eye was turned inward (esotropia) before the cover was placed.

### Question 35

What is the critical period for the development of amblyopia?

A) Birth to 3 months
B) 3 months to 3 years
C) Birth to 6-8 years
D) 6-12 years

**Correct Answer: C) Birth to 6-8 years**

**Explanation:** The critical period for amblyopia development extends from birth to approximately 6-8 years of age, with the highest risk during the first 3 years. During this time, the visual system requires clear, focused visual input from both eyes to develop normal visual acuity and binocular function.

### Question 36

Which of the following is characteristic of convergence insufficiency?

A) High AC/A ratio
B) Receded near point of convergence
C) Esotropia at near
D) Exophoria greater at distance than near

**Correct Answer: B) Receded near point of convergence**

**Explanation:** Convergence insufficiency (CI) is characterized by an inability to maintain adequate convergence for near tasks. Key findings include a receded near point of convergence (NPC), typically > 6cm, exophoria greater at near than at distance, and reduced positive fusional vergence amplitudes.

### Question 37

A 4-year-old child presents with 20/40 visual acuity in each eye, normal eye alignment, and no significant refractive error. What is the MOST appropriate management?

A) Prescribe glasses
B) Patch one eye
C) Reassure parents and recheck in 6 months
D) Refer for neurological evaluation

**Correct Answer: C) Reassure parents and recheck in 6 months**

**Explanation:** For a 4-year-old child, 20/40 visual acuity is within normal limits for age. Visual acuity in children develops gradually: 20/50 by age 1, 20/30-20/40 by age 3-4, and approaching 20/20 by age 5-6. In the absence of other findings, this level of acuity is age-appropriate.

### Question 38

What is the normal AC/A (accommodative convergence to accommodation) ratio?

A) 1:1
B) 3:1
C) 5:1
D) 6:1

**Correct Answer: B) 3:1**

**Explanation:** The normal AC/A ratio is approximately 3:1 to 5:1, meaning that for every diopter of accommodation, the eyes converge 3-5 prism diopters. A high AC/A ratio is associated with accommodative esotropia, while a low AC/A ratio may be seen in convergence insufficiency.

### Question 39

Which of the following topical agents is a carbonic anhydrase inhibitor used in glaucoma treatment?

B) Dorzolamide

**Correct Answer: B) Dorzolamide**

### Question 42

Which mydriatic agent has the longest duration of action?

A) Phenylephrine 2.5%
B) Tropicamide 1%
C) Cyclopentolate 1%
D) Atropine 1%

**Correct Answer: D) Atropine 1%**

**Explanation:** Atropine has the longest duration of action among mydriatic agents, with mydriasis lasting 7-14 days and cycloplegia lasting up to 2 weeks. This is because atropine binds strongly to muscarinic receptors and is slowly eliminated from the eye.

### Question 43

Which of the following is a prostaglandin analog used in glaucoma treatment?

A) Brimonidine
B) Timolol
C) Latanoprost
D) Acetazolamide

**Correct Answer: C) Latanoprost**

**Explanation:** Latanoprost is a prostaglandin analog that increases uveoscleral outflow of aqueous humor, reducing intraocular pressure by 25-35%. It is typically dosed once daily at bedtime and is often the first-line treatment for open-angle glaucoma.

### Question 44

What is the mechanism of action of pilocarpine in the treatment of angle-closure glaucoma?

A) Decreases aqueous production
B) Increases uveoscleral outflow
C) Causes miosis and opens the angle
D) Decreases episcleral venous pressure

**Correct Answer: C) Causes miosis and opens the angle**

**Explanation:** Pilocarpine is a muscarinic agonist that causes miosis (pupil constriction) and contraction of the ciliary muscle. In angle-closure glaucoma, the miosis pulls the iris away from the trabecular meshwork, opening the angle and allowing aqueous outflow.

Which visual field defect is characteristic of a pituitary adenoma compressing the optic chiasm?

**Correct Answer: A) Right optic nerve pathology**

### Question 50

Which cranial nerve is MOST commonly affected in diabetic ophthalmoplegia?

B) Cranial nerve IV

D) Cranial nerve VII

**Correct Answer: C) Cranial nerve VI**

**Explanation:** Cranial nerve VI (abducens nerve) is the most commonly affected cranial nerve in diabetic ophthalmoplegia, resulting in ipsilateral lateral rectus weakness and esotropia. The microvascular nature of diabetic neuropathy preferentially affects the longer intracranial course of the sixth nerve.

### Question 53

A patient with age-related macular degeneration has a central scotoma and uses an eccentric viewing technique. What is this technique called?

A) Eccentric fixation
B) Preferred retinal locus (PRL)
C) Fixation displacement
D) Parafoveal viewing

**Correct Answer: B) Preferred retinal locus (PRL)**

**Explanation:** Preferred retinal locus (PRL) describes the eccentric viewing technique employed by patients with central scotomas. When the fovea is damaged, patients develop an alternative fixation point on a healthy area of the retina, which becomes the new "pseudo-fovea" for visual tasks.

### Question 54

What is the legal definition of blindness in most jurisdictions?

A) Visual acuity of 20/200 or worse in the better eye
B) Visual acuity of 20/400 or worse in either eye
C) Visual field of less than 30 degrees
D) Both A and C

**Correct Answer: D) Both A and C**

**Explanation:** Legal blindness is defined as: (1) Best-corrected visual acuity of 20/200 or worse in the better eye, OR (2) Visual field constriction to 20 degrees or less in the better eye. This definition is used for legal and social service purposes.

### Question 55

Which of the following is NOT an appropriate low vision aid for a patient with advanced glaucoma and peripheral field loss?

A) Tinted lenses
B) Handheld magnifier
C) Field expansion prisms
D) High-plus spectacles

**Correct Answer: D) High-plus spectacles**
