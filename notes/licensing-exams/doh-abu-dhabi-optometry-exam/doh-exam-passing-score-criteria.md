# DOH Exam Passing Score & Criteria

## Introduction

The Department of Health (DOH) Abu Dhabi licensing examination for optometrists is a crucial gateway for healthcare professionals seeking to practice optometry in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates. As the regulatory body overseeing healthcare standards in the capital, DOH Abu Dhabi maintains rigorous examination and licensing procedures to ensure only qualified professionals provide eye care services to the community. This comprehensive guide provides detailed information about passing score requirements, competency thresholds, examination structure, and license issuance procedures specific to the DOH Abu Dhabi optometry examination.

Understanding the DOH examination framework is essential for international optometrists planning to work in Abu Dhabi, as well as local graduates seeking professional licensure. The examination is designed to assess clinical knowledge, diagnostic skills, and professional competency across all areas of optometric practice.

## 1. Passing Score Requirements

### Minimum Passing Score

The DOH Abu Dhabi Optometry examination requires candidates to achieve a minimum passing score of **60%** to qualify for licensure consideration. This threshold has been established through comprehensive analysis of competency standards and aligns with international best practices for optometry licensing examinations.

The 60% passing standard reflects the minimum knowledge and skill level necessary to provide safe and effective patient care. This standard is regularly reviewed by the DOH Professional Licensing Department in consultation with optometry subject matter experts and healthcare quality committees.

### Score Distribution Across Competency Areas

The examination is structured with questions distributed across multiple competency domains. Each domain carries specific weight in the overall score calculation:

| Competency Domain | Weight Percentage | Description |
|-------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Clinical Optometry & Refraction | 30% | Primary eye examination, refraction techniques, binocular vision |
| Ocular Disease & Pathology | 25% | Disease identification, management protocols, referral criteria |
| Optical Dispensing & Technology | 15% | Lens design, ophthalmic optics, frame selection, instrumentation |
| Professional Ethics & Regulations | 15% | DOH regulations, professional conduct, patient rights |
| Special Populations & Therapeutics | 15% | Pediatric optometry, geriatric care, therapeutic agents |

### Section-Specific Minimum Requirements

Beyond the overall passing score, candidates must demonstrate minimum competency in critical clinical areas:

- **Clinical Optometry & Refraction**: Minimum 50% required in this section
- **Ocular Disease & Pathology**: Minimum 55% required (higher due to patient safety implications)
- **Professional Ethics & Regulations**: Minimum 60% required (reflects importance of regulatory compliance)

Failure to meet section-specific minimums, even with an overall passing score, may result in conditional licensing requirements or additional assessment.

### Examination Format and Scoring

The DOH Optometry examination format includes:

**Written Component:**
- 100 multiple-choice questions (MCQs)
- Single best answer format
- Clinical scenario-based questions included
- No negative marking for incorrect answers
- Duration: 2 hours

**Clinical Assessment Component (where applicable):**
- Practical skills demonstration
- Patient examination scenarios
- Case presentation requirements
- May be required based on initial screening

### Score Validity Period

A passing DOH examination score maintains validity for **2 years** from the examination date. During this period, candidates must complete all licensing requirements including document verification and primary source verification. If licensing is not completed within the validity period, candidates must retake the examination.

## 2. Score Calculation Methodology

### Raw Score Computation

The raw examination score is calculated using a straightforward formula:

**Raw Score = (Number of Correct Answers / Total Questions) × 100**

For example, a candidate answering 72 questions correctly out of 100 would receive a raw score of 72%.

### Weighted Score Calculation

The weighted score considers the importance of different competency domains:

**Weighted Score = Σ (Section Score × Section Weight)**

This methodology ensures that critical clinical areas contribute appropriately to the overall assessment of competency.

### Domain-Specific Scoring Breakdown

**Clinical Optometry & Refraction Domain (30%):**
- Refraction techniques: 10 points
- Binocular vision assessment: 8 points
- Visual function testing: 7 points
- Contact lens fundamentals: 5 points

**Ocular Disease & Pathology Domain (25%):**
- Anterior segment diseases: 10 points
- Posterior segment conditions: 8 points
- Systemic disease manifestations: 4 points
- Emergency management: 3 points

**Optical Dispensing & Technology Domain (15%):**
- Ophthalmic optics principles: 6 points
- Lens design and selection: 5 points
- Instrumentation and technology: 4 points

**Professional Ethics & Regulations Domain (15%):**
- DOH regulatory framework: 6 points
- Professional conduct standards: 5 points
- Patient rights and consent: 4 points

**Special Populations & Therapeutics Domain (15%):**
- Pediatric optometry: 5 points
- Geriatric considerations: 5 points
- Therapeutic pharmaceutical agents: 5 points

### Score Rounding Policy

- Scores are rounded to the nearest whole number
- Scores of 59.5% and above are rounded up to 60% (passing)
- Scores below 59.5% are rounded down (failing)
- No additional points awarded for partial answers

### Official Score Report Contents

The DOH provides comprehensive score reports including:

- Overall percentage score
- Pass/fail determination
- Section-by-section performance breakdown
- Comparison to minimum passing standards
- Percentile ranking among examination cohort
- Areas of strength and improvement recommendations
- Next steps guidance for successful candidates

## 3. Competency Thresholds

### Definition and Purpose

Competency thresholds represent the minimum acceptable level of knowledge, skills, and professional judgment required to provide safe optometric care in Abu Dhabi. These thresholds are established through rigorous analysis by DOH's Professional Licensing Department in collaboration with academic institutions and practicing specialists.

### Competency Level Classification

| Level | Score Range | Interpretation | Practice Implications |
|-------|-------------|----------------|----------------------|
| Exemplary | 85-100% | Superior knowledge and clinical judgment | Eligible for advanced practice consideration |
| Proficient | 70-84% | Strong competency across all domains | Standard licensing pathway |
| Competent | 60-69% | Meets minimum standards | Standard licensing with recommended mentorship |
| Marginal | 55-59% | Near threshold, significant gaps identified | May require supervised practice period |
| Below Competent | Below 55% | Does not meet standards | Examination retake required |

### Clinical Competency Standards

**Diagnostic Competency Requirements:**
Candidates must demonstrate ability to:
- Perform comprehensive eye examinations independently
- Accurately measure and correct refractive errors
- Identify common ocular diseases and conditions
- Recognize signs requiring specialist referral
- Interpret diagnostic test results correctly

**Patient Care Competency Requirements:**
Candidates must demonstrate ability to:
- Develop appropriate treatment plans
- Communicate effectively with patients
- Maintain professional documentation
- Apply ethical decision-making in clinical scenarios
- Manage emergency eye care situations

### Domain-Specific Competency Expectations

**Refractive Assessment Competency:**
- Accurate retinoscopy and subjective refraction
- Binocular balance techniques
- Near vision assessment
- Prescription writing standards
- Patient communication regarding correction options

**Ocular Disease Competency:**
- Differential diagnosis of red eye conditions
- Glaucoma screening and identification
- Diabetic retinopathy recognition
- Management of anterior segment disorders
- Emergency eye condition triage

**Professional Practice Competency:**
- DOH regulatory compliance understanding
- Patient confidentiality maintenance
- Informed consent procedures
- Record-keeping standards
- Professional boundary maintenance

### Borderline Performance Review

Candidates scoring between 58-60% receive additional review:

1. Section performance analysis
2. Clinical case management assessment
3. Professional judgment evaluation
4. Possible oral examination component
5. Committee determination on licensure eligibility

## 4. Examination Failure Procedures

### Immediate Post-Failure Process

Candidates who do not achieve the passing score will:

1. Receive detailed score report within 14 business days
2. Obtain section-specific performance feedback
3. Access comparative analytics showing areas of weakness
4. Receive personalized study recommendations
5. Be provided with retake timeline information

### Score Verification Request

Candidates may request score verification within **21 days** of receiving results:

- **Verification Fee**: AED 250 (non-refundable)
- **Process**: Manual verification of answer key application and score calculation
- **Timeline**: Results within 10 business days
- **Scope**: Administrative verification only, not re-grading of responses

### Formal Appeal Process

For candidates with extenuating circumstances affecting examination performance:

1. **Appeal Submission**: Written appeal within 30 days of results
2. **Supporting Documentation**: Medical certificates, emergency documentation, technical issue reports
3. **Appeal Fee**: AED 500 (refunded if appeal successful)
4. **Review Committee**: Professional Licensing Appeal Committee
5. **Decision Timeline**: Within 21 business days
6. **Final Determination**: Binding decision communicated in writing

### Failure Categorization and Recommendations

**Marginal Failure (55-59%):**
- Recommended focused study on identified weak areas
- Suggested minimum 4-week preparation period
- Access to DOH-approved remediation resources
- May qualify for conditional retake

**Moderate Failure (45-54%):**
- Recommended comprehensive review of all domains
- Suggested enrollment in preparatory courses
- Minimum 8-week preparation period advised
- Mentor matching recommended

**Significant Failure (Below 45%):**
- Recommended complete educational program review
- May require additional clinical training documentation
- Minimum 12-week preparation period advised
- Consider formal education refresher program

### Available Support Resources

DOH provides access to various support mechanisms:
- Approved preparation courses and study materials
- Practice examination portals
- Online learning modules
- Mentor matching programs
- Study group facilitation
- Professional development workshops

## 5. Re-examination Policies

### Waiting Period Requirements

DOH implements structured waiting periods between examination attempts:

| Attempt Number | Minimum Waiting Period | Additional Requirements |
|---------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| 1st Retake | 30 days | Self-assessment of weak areas |
| 2nd Retake | 60 days | Recommended preparatory course |
| 3rd Retake | 90 days | Mandatory preparation course completion certificate |
| 4th Retake | 6 months | Full re-application and credential review |

### Maximum Attempt Limitations

- Candidates are permitted a maximum of **4 examination attempts** within a 3-year period
- After 4 unsuccessful attempts, candidates must:
  - Wait minimum 12 months before reapplying
  - Complete additional educational requirements as specified
  - Provide evidence of professional development activities
  - Undergo credential re-evaluation

### Re-examination Fee Structure

| Attempt | Examination Fee (AED) | Additional Costs |
|---------|----------------------|------------------|
| Initial | 1,200 | Application processing: 600 |
| 1st Retake | 1,200 | None |
| 2nd Retake | 1,400 | None |
| 3rd Retake | 1,600 | Preparation course: Variable |
| 4th Retake | 2,000 | Full re-application: 600 |

### Re-examination Application Process

1. **Online Portal Application**: Through DOH Sheryan/TAMM platform
2. **Waiting Period Verification**: System confirmation of elapsed waiting period
3. **Document Update**: Submission of any updated credentials if applicable
4. **Fee Payment**: Through approved payment methods
5. **Scheduling**: Selection from available examination dates
6. **Confirmation**: Email confirmation with examination details

### Special Re-examination Circumstances

**Technical Failure During Examination:**
- No waiting period required
- No additional fee charged
- Priority scheduling for retake
- Formal apology from DOH

**Medical Emergency During Examination:**
- Documented medical emergency
- Rescheduling without penalty
- Fee deferred to new date
- Priority scheduling upon recovery

## 6. License Issuance Process

### Post-Examination Requirements

Upon achieving a passing examination score, candidates must complete the following for license issuance:

**Primary Source Verification (PSV):**
- Educational credential verification through approved PSV provider
- Professional qualification authentication
- Experience verification from previous employers
- Timeline: 3-8 weeks depending on institution response

**Good Standing Certificates:**
- Required from all jurisdictions where previously licensed
- Must be issued within 3 months of submission
- Direct verification by DOH preferred
- Timeline: Variable by issuing authority

**Background Screening:**
- Criminal background check from country of residence
- Professional conduct verification
- Disciplinary history review
- Timeline: 2-4 weeks

### Required Documentation for License Application

| Document | Requirements |
|----------|-------------|
| Valid Passport | Minimum 6 months validity, clear copy |
| Passport Photograph | White background, professional attire, recent |
| Educational Degree | Attested and verified, official transcripts |
| Experience Certificates | Minimum 2 years post-qualification, attested |
| DOH Examination Result | Official passing score certificate |
| Good Standing Certificate | From all previous licensing authorities |
| Medical Fitness Certificate | From UAE-approved medical facility |
| Professional Indemnity Insurance | Minimum AED 500,000 coverage |
| Language Proficiency | IELTS 6.0 or equivalent (if applicable) |

### License Application Processing Timeline

| Processing Stage | Duration |
|-----------------|----------|
| Initial Document Review | 5-7 business days |
| Primary Source Verification | 3-8 weeks |
| Background Screening | 2-4 weeks |
| Committee Review | 5-10 business days |
| License Issuance | 3-5 business days |
| **Total Estimated Timeline** | **6-12 weeks** |

### License Categories and Privileges

**Full Practicing License:**
- Complete scope of optometric practice
- Independent practice privileges
- All diagnostic and prescriptive authorities within scope
- Eligible for facility ownership/partnership

**Supervised Practice License:**
- Practice under DOH-licensed optometrist supervision
- Limited procedures initially
- 6-month supervision period
- Conversion to full license upon satisfactory evaluation

**Temporary License:**
- Limited duration (up to 6 months)
- Specific project or locum coverage
- Restricted to approved facility
- Not renewable beyond initial period

### License Fee Structure

| License Type | Fee (AED) | Validity Period |
|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| New License Application | 3,500 | 1 year |
| License Renewal | 2,500 | 1 year |
| License Amendment | 500 | N/A |
| License Replacement | 200 | N/A |
| Late Renewal Penalty | 100/month | N/A |

## 7. Practice Permit and Scope of Practice

### Practice Permit Overview

The DOH practice permit authorizes optometrists to practice at specific healthcare facilities in Abu Dhabi. The permit serves as official documentation of professional authorization and must be displayed at the primary practice location.

### Authorized Practice Scope

**Diagnostic Procedures Authorized:**
- Comprehensive eye and vision examinations
- Visual acuity and refractive assessments
- Binocular vision evaluation
- Color vision testing
- Visual field assessment
- Intraocular pressure measurement
- Anterior and posterior segment examination
- Optical coherence tomography (OCT)
- Corneal topography and tomography
- Retinal imaging and photography

**Prescriptive Authority:**
- Spectacle and contact lens prescriptions
- Non-prescription ophthalmic products
- Therapeutic pharmaceutical agents (as per DOH-approved formulary)

**Therapeutic Procedures:**
- Superficial foreign body removal
- Contact lens fitting and management
- Vision therapy programs
- Low vision assessment and rehabilitation

### Practice Limitations

DOH-licensed optometrists must NOT:
- Perform surgical procedures
- Administer injectable medications
- Prescribe systemic/oral medications
- Manage conditions requiring ophthalmologist care
- Perform laser procedures
- Provide treatment beyond training and certification

### Mandatory Referral Conditions

Immediate ophthalmologist referral required for:
- Glaucoma (suspected or confirmed)
- Cataract affecting visual function
- Retinal detachment or pathology
- Uveitis and intraocular inflammation
- Optic nerve abnormalities
- Ocular tumors
- Diabetic retinopathy (moderate to severe)
- Wet macular degeneration
- Neuro-ophthalmic conditions
- Pediatric surgical conditions

## 8. Continuing Professional Development Requirements

### Annual CPD Requirements

DOH-licensed optometrists must complete continuing professional development annually:

| CPD Category | Minimum Hours | Maximum Credit |
|--------------|---------------|----------------|
| Clinical Optometry | 12 hours | No limit |
| Ocular Disease | 8 hours | No limit |
| Professional Ethics | 4 hours | No limit |
| Practice Management | 3 hours | No limit |
| **Annual Total Requirement** | **27 hours** | - |

### Approved CPD Activities

**Category A - Formal Education:**
- International conferences and symposia
- Accredited workshops and seminars
- University continuing education programs
- Online accredited courses

**Category B - Professional Activities:**
- Journal club participation
- Case presentations at professional meetings
- Peer review activities
- Research publication and presentation

**Category C - Self-Directed Learning:**
- Verified online learning modules
- Journal reading with assessment
- Educational podcasts with completion verification

### CPD Documentation Requirements

- Certificate of completion from approved provider
- Activity description and learning objectives
- Hours claimed and category classification
- Provider accreditation information
- Date and location of activity

### CPD Non-Compliance Consequences

**First Year Non-Compliance:**
- Warning notice issued
- Required remediation plan
- Additional 15 CPD hours required

**Second Year Non-Compliance:**
- License suspension (3 months)
- Mandatory CPD completion
- Reinstatement fee: AED 1,500

**Third Year Non-Compliance:**
- License revocation
- 1-year waiting period for reapplication
- Full re-examination may be required
