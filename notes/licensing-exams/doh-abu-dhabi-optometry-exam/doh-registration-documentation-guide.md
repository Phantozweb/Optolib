# DOH Registration & Documentation Guide

## Comprehensive Guide for Abu Dhabi Optometry Licensing Examination

This comprehensive guide provides detailed information about the registration process, documentation requirements, and procedural steps for the Department of Health (DOH) Abu Dhabi Optometry Examination. Follow this guide carefully to ensure a smooth application process.

---

## 1. Pre-Registration Requirements

### Eligibility Criteria

Before initiating the DOH Abu Dhabi Optometry registration process, candidates must meet the following fundamental requirements:

#### Educational Qualifications
- **Bachelor's Degree in Optometry**: A minimum of a four-year bachelor's degree in optometry from a recognized and accredited institution is mandatory
- **Program Accreditation**: The optometry program must be accredited by the relevant regulatory body in the country of origin
- **Clinical Training**: The degree program must include substantial clinical training components (minimum 1,000 hours of supervised clinical practice)

#### Professional Experience
- **Clinical Experience**: A minimum of two (2) years of post-licensure clinical experience in optometry practice is required
- **Experience Verification**: All experience must be documented and verifiable through official employment letters
- **Continuous Practice**: Recent practice within the last two years is preferred; gaps in practice may require additional assessment

#### Language Proficiency
- **English Language Requirements**: Candidates whose primary education was not in English must demonstrate proficiency through:
  - IELTS Academic: Minimum overall band score of 6.5, with no individual band below 6.0
  - TOEFL iBT: Minimum total score of 79, with minimum scores of 19 in Reading, 20 in Listening, 21 in Speaking, and 19 in Writing
  - Alternative: Proof that primary education (optometry degree) was conducted entirely in English

#### Age Requirements
- Minimum age: 21 years
- Maximum age: No upper limit, but health fitness requirements apply

### Pre-Registration Checklist

| Requirement | Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Bachelor's Degree in Optometry | ☐ | Must be 4-year minimum |
| Valid Professional License | ☐ | From country of practice |

| Passport Photos | ☐ | White background |

## 2. Document Verification Process

### Overview of Verification

The document verification process is a critical component of the DOH licensing pathway. All submitted documents undergo rigorous verification to ensure authenticity and accuracy. This process typically takes 4-8 weeks depending on the complexity and origin of documents.

### Document Authentication Steps

#### Step 1: Notarization
1. Have all educational documents notarized by a licensed notary public in the country of origin
2. Ensure notarization includes the notary's seal, signature, and commission expiration date
3. Documents must be notarized in the same country where they were issued

#### Step 2: Authentication by Relevant Authorities
1. **Educational Documents**: Must be authenticated by:
   - The issuing institution (Registrar's office)
   - Ministry of Education in the country of origin
   - Ministry of Foreign Affairs in the country of origin
   - UAE Embassy in the country of origin

2. **Experience Certificates**: Must be authenticated by:
   - The employing organization
   - Labor department or equivalent authority
   - Ministry of Foreign Affairs
   - UAE Embassy

#### Step 3: UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) Attestation
1. Submit authenticated documents to MOFA in the UAE
2. Pay the attestation fees (approximately AED 150-200 per document)
3. Processing time: 2-3 working days

### Document Translation Requirements

- All documents not in English or Arabic must be translated by a MOFA-authorized translator
- Translations must include:
  - Translator's official stamp and signature
  - Statement of accuracy
  - Date of translation
- Both original and translated documents must be submitted

### Common Documentation Issues

| Issue | Impact | Resolution |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Missing attestation chain | Application rejection | Complete full attestation chain |
| Expired documents | Verification failure | Obtain renewed documents |
| Name discrepancies | Delay in processing | Provide affidavit or legal name change document |
| Incomplete translations | Rejection | Use MOFA-authorized translator |
| Faded stamps/seals | Verification failure | Obtain fresh copies |

## 3. Educational Credential Requirements

### Degree Requirements

The DOH Abu Dhabi has specific requirements for optometry educational credentials:

#### Bachelor of Optometry (B.Optom/B.Sc Optometry)
- **Duration**: Minimum 4 years of full-time study
- **Credit Hours**: Minimum 120-140 credit hours
- **Clinical Components**: Must include structured clinical rotations in:
  - General optometry practice
  - Contact lens fitting and management
  - Pediatric optometry
  - Binocular vision assessment
  - Ocular disease diagnosis and management
  - Low vision rehabilitation

#### Required Coursework
The optometry degree must cover the following subjects:

| Subject Area | Minimum Credit Hours |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Ocular Anatomy & Physiology | 3 credits |
| Visual Optics | 4 credits |
| Geometric Optics | 3 credits |
| Clinical Optometry | 6 credits |
| Ophthalmic Optics | 3 credits |
| Binocular Vision | 3 credits |
| Contact Lenses | 4 credits |
| Ocular Disease | 4 credits |
| Pediatric Optometry | 2 credits |
| Pharmacology | 3 credits |
| Research Methods | 2 credits |

### Transcripts Requirements

1. **Official Transcripts**: Must be sent directly from the institution to DOH or DataFlow
2. **Complete Academic Record**: Must show all courses, grades, and credits
3. **Degree Certificate**: Official degree certificate with graduation date
4. **Course Description**: Detailed course descriptions may be required for verification

### Educational Credential Evaluation

If your degree is from a non-UAE institution, DOH may require credential evaluation through:
- **DataFlow Group**: Primary source verification
- **MOHESR (Ministry of Higher Education)**: For equivalence assessment
- **Recognized Evaluation Services**: Depending on country of origin

### Degrees from Specific Countries

#### United States/Canada
- Doctor of Optometry (O.D.) degree from accredited institution
- NBEO/NBOD certification verification required
- State license verification required

#### United Kingdom/Australia
- Bachelor of Optometry (Hons) or equivalent
- GOC/BOA registration verification
- Clinical competency assessment

#### India
- Bachelor of Optometry (4-year program)
- COA (Council of Optometry) registration verification
- MCI/NMC equivalence may be required

#### Philippines
- Doctor of Optometry degree
- PRC license verification
- Board certification documents

## 4. Experience Documentation

### Experience Requirements

The DOH requires documented evidence of professional experience in optometry practice:

#### Minimum Experience
- **Duration**: 2 years minimum post-licensure experience
- **Type**: Clinical optometry practice (not training or internship)
- **Recency**: Experience within the last 5 years preferred

• Comprehensive eye examinations

• Contact lens fitting and follow-up care

• Low vision assessment and rehabilitation

### Experience Verification Process

1. **Submit Experience Letters**: From all employers in the last 5 years
2. **Employer Verification**: DOH/DataFlow will contact employers directly
3. **Gap Explanation**: Any employment gaps must be explained with supporting documents
4. **Self-Employment**: Must provide business registration, client testimonials, and tax records

### Documenting Different Practice Settings

#### Hospital-Based Practice
- Employment letter from HR department
- Department head recommendation letter
- List of procedures performed
- Patient volume statistics

#### Private Practice
- Employer letter with practice details
- Clinic registration/license copy
- Scope of practice documentation

#### Academic/Research Positions
- Employment letter from institution
- Teaching load and clinical hours
- Research contributions (if applicable)

## 5. Good Standing Certificates

### What is a Good Standing Certificate?

A Good Standing Certificate (also known as Certificate of Standing, Letter of Good Standing, or Professional Standing Certificate) confirms that an optometrist:
- Holds a valid, current license
- Has no disciplinary actions or restrictions
- Is authorized to practice optometry
- Has no pending complaints or investigations

### Requirements for Good Standing Certificate

#### From Country of Primary Qualification
1. Obtain from the optometry licensing authority
2. Must be issued within the last 6 months
3. Must include:
   - License number and issue date
   - Current status (active, in good standing)
   - Any restrictions or limitations
   - Disciplinary history (or statement of no disciplinary action)
   - Expiration date of current license

#### From All Countries of Practice
If you have practiced optometry in multiple countries, you need Good Standing Certificates from:
- Country of primary qualification
- All countries where you have held a license
- Current country of practice

### Obtaining Good Standing Certificates by Region

#### United States
- Contact State Board of Optometry
- Request via NPLEX or state-specific portal
- Processing time: 2-4 weeks
- Fee: $25-$100 per certificate

#### United Kingdom
- General Optical Council (GOC)
- Online request through GOC portal
- Processing time: 5-10 working days
- Fee: Approximately £35

#### Australia
- Optometry Board of Australia (AHPRA)
- Request via AHPRA portal
- Processing time: 10-15 working days
- Fee: AUD 55

#### India
- State Optometry Council
- Application form with ID proof
- Processing time: 2-4 weeks
- Fee: Varies by state

#### Philippines
- Professional Regulation Commission (PRC)
- Request via PRC portal
- Processing time: 1-2 weeks
- Fee: PHP 200-500

### Authentication Requirements

Good Standing Certificates must be:
1. Issued on official letterhead
2. Signed by an authorized official
3. Include the organization's seal/stamp
4. Attested according to the document verification chain
5. Translated if not in English or Arabic

## 7. Primary Source Verification (PSV)

Primary Source Verification (PSV) is the process of verifying credentials directly with the original issuing source, without relying on copies or secondary sources. This ensures the highest level of document authenticity.

### PSV Requirements for DOH

All the following must undergo PSV:

#### Educational Documents
- Degree certificates
- Academic transcripts
- Professional diplomas
- Course completion certificates

#### Professional Documents
- License certificates
- Good Standing Certificates
- Board certifications
- Registration documents

## 9. Prometric Scheduling Process

### About the Prometric Exam

The DOH Optometry examination is administered through Prometric testing centers. The exam assesses knowledge, clinical skills, and professional competencies required for optometry practice in Abu Dhabi.

### Exam Format

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Number of Questions | 150 multiple-choice questions |
| Duration | 3 hours |
| Passing Score | 60-65% (may vary) |
| Language | English |
| Question Type | Single best answer |

### Scheduling Your Exam

#### Step 1: Receive Authorization
1. Complete all document verification
2. Receive exam eligibility from DOH
3. Check email for Prometric authorization number

3. Enter your authorization number
4. Create personal profile

#### Step 3: Select Test Center
1. Choose preferred location
2. View available dates
3. Consider travel arrangements

## 11. Rescheduling Policies

### Standard Rescheduling Rules

Understanding the rescheduling policies helps avoid unnecessary fees and complications:

#### Rescheduling Timeline and Fees

| When Rescheduled | Fee (AED) | Fee (USD) |
|------------------|-----------|-----------|
| More than 30 days before | Free | $0 |
| 15-30 days before | 150 | $40 |
| 5-14 days before | 300 | $80 |
| Less than 5 days before | 400 | $110 |
| Same day/No-show | Full exam fee forfeited | - |

### Rescheduling Process

#### Online Rescheduling
1. Log into Prometric account
2. Select current appointment
3. Choose "Reschedule" option
4. Select new date and time
5. Pay applicable fee
6. Receive new confirmation

#### Phone Rescheduling
1. Call Prometric customer service
2. Provide confirmation number
3. Request new date
4. Pay applicable fees
5. Receive confirmation via email

### Conditions for Free Rescheduling

DOH may allow free rescheduling under the following circumstances:

1. **Medical Emergency**
   - Hospital documentation required
   - Medical certificate from recognized facility
   - Request within 48 hours

2. **Bereavement**
   - Death certificate required
   - Immediate family member only
   - Request within 48 hours

3. **Technical Issues**
   - Prometric center technical failure
   - Documentation from test center
   - Automatic rescheduling

4. **Force Majeure**
   - Natural disasters
   - Civil unrest
   - Government restrictions

### Maximum Rescheduling Attempts

- Maximum 3 reschedules per authorization
- After 3 attempts, must reapply to DOH
- Original authorization valid for 12 months

### Missed Exam Policy

If you miss your scheduled exam:

1. **Without Notice**
   - Full fee forfeited
   - Must reapply and pay full fee again
   - New authorization required

2. **With Valid Reason**
   - Submit documentation within 48 hours
   - May receive partial credit toward new exam
   - Subject to DOH review

## 12. Post-Exam Procedures

### Immediately After the Exam

#### Score Reporting
- **Preliminary Score**: May be available immediately (unofficial)
- **Official Results**: Released within 5-10 business days
- **Score Report**: Detailed breakdown sent via email

#### Results Notification
1. Log into DOH portal to view results
2. Check email for official notification
3. Download score report

### If You Pass

#### License Issuance Process

**Step 1: License Application**
1. Submit license application form
2. Pay license issuance fee (AED 3,000)
3. Provide any additional requested documents

**Step 2: Medical Fitness Test**
1. Schedule at DOH-approved medical center
2. Complete blood tests and physical examination
3. Obtain medical fitness certificate

**Step 3: Professional Liability Insurance**
1. Obtain malpractice insurance
2. Minimum coverage: AED 500,000
3. Submit policy document to DOH

**Step 4: License Collection**
1. Receive notification for license collection
2. Visit DOH office with original documents
3. Sign license agreement
4. Receive license card and certificate

#### License Validity
- **Initial License**: Valid for 1 year
- **Renewal**: Annual renewal required
- **CME Requirements**: Minimum 20 CME hours per year

### If You Fail

#### Re-examination Policy
1. **Waiting Period**: 45 days minimum
2. **Maximum Attempts**: 3 attempts within 12 months
3. **After 3 Failures**: Must reapply with additional training evidence

#### Steps to Retake
1. Wait for official fail notification
2. Pay re-examination fee
3. Schedule new exam date
4. Review weak areas from score report

#### Support Resources
- DOH candidate support services
- Exam preparation courses
- Study groups and forums

### License Maintenance

#### Annual Requirements
| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| CME Hours | Minimum 20 hours annually |
| Practice Hours | Minimum 200 hours annually |
| Insurance | Maintain professional liability coverage |
| Good Standing | No disciplinary actions |

#### License Renewal Process
1. Submit renewal application 60 days before expiry
2. Provide CME certificates
3. Pay renewal fee (AED 1,500)
4. Submit current practice evidence
5. Receive renewed license

### Practice Rights and Limitations

#### Scope of Practice
Licensed optometrists in Abu Dhabi may:
- Perform comprehensive eye examinations
- Prescribe corrective lenses (glasses and contact lenses)
- Diagnose and manage common ocular conditions
- Prescribe therapeutic pharmaceutical agents (as authorized)
- Refer to ophthalmologists when necessary
- Provide vision therapy and rehabilitation

#### Limitations
- No surgical procedures
- No injection administration (without additional certification)
- Prescription limitations for certain medications
- Mandatory referral for surgical conditions
