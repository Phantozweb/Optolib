# CANADA Clinical Case Questions - Scenarios

## Practice Questions for Canadian OEBC

### Question 1
A 50-year-old patient presents with gradual vision loss. IOP is 26 mmHg with optic disc cupping. The most likely diagnosis is:

A. Ocular hypertension
B. Primary open-angle glaucoma
C. Normal tension glaucoma
D. Angle-closure glaucoma

**Correct: B**

### Question 2
Which medication increases uveoscleral outflow?

A. Timolol
B. Latanoprost
C. Acetazolamide
D. Pilocarpine

**Correct: B**

### Question 3
Contraindication for beta-blockers includes:

A. Diabetes
B. Asthma
C. Hyperopia
D. Dry eye

**Correct: B**

### Question 4
Best cycloplegic for infant refraction is:

A. Tropicamide
B. Cyclopentolate
C. Atropine
D. Phenylephrine

**Correct: C**

### Question 5
Immediate step for contact lens-related corneal ulcer:

A. Start antibiotic drops
B. Discontinue lens wear
C. Change lens solution
D. Use steroid drops

**Correct: B**
