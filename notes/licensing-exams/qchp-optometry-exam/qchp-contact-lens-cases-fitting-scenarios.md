# QCHP Contact Lens Cases - Fitting Scenarios

## Clinical Cases for Qatar QCHP

### Case 1: Diabetic Retinopathy
**Patient:** 58-year-old diabetic
**Findings:** Microaneurysms, hemorrhages, hard exudates
**Diagnosis:** Non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy
**Management:** Referral to ophthalmology, glycemic control

### Case 2: Acute Red Eye
**Patient:** 35-year-old contact lens wearer
**Findings:** Red eye, pain, discharge, corneal infiltrate
**Diagnosis:** Microbial keratitis
**Management:** Discontinue lenses, urgent ophthalmology referral

### Case 3: Pediatric Amblyopia
**Patient:** 5-year-old with reduced vision
**Findings:** Anisometropia, reduced stereopsis
**Diagnosis:** Anisometropic amblyopia
**Management:** Glasses, patching therapy

### Case 4: Glaucoma Suspect
**Patient:** 60-year-old with family history
**Findings:** IOP 24 mmHg, C/D 0.6
**Diagnosis:** Glaucoma suspect
**Management:** Visual fields, OCT, monitoring

### Case 5: Sudden Vision Loss
**Patient:** 65-year-old with sudden painless vision loss
**Findings:** Cherry-red spot, pale retina
**Diagnosis:** Central retinal artery occlusion
**Management:** Emergency referral
