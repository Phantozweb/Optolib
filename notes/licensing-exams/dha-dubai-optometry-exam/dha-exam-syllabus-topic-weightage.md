# DHA Exam Syllabus & Topic Weightage

## Executive Summary

The Dubai Health Authority (DHA) Optometry Licensing Examination is a comprehensive assessment designed to evaluate the competency of optometrists seeking to practice in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. This document provides a detailed breakdown of the examination syllabus, topic weightages, and identifies high-yield areas that candidates should prioritize during their preparation. Understanding the syllabus structure and topic distribution is essential for effective study planning and maximizing examination performance.

The examination consists of 100 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) administered over 2.5 hours, with a passing score requirement of 60%. The content is strategically distributed across multiple competency domains, each carrying specific weightage reflecting its clinical importance and relevance to optometric practice in the UAE healthcare context.

## 1. Overall Topic Distribution & Weightage

### Primary Competency Areas

| Competency Domain | Weightage | Number of Questions | Importance Level |
|-------------------|-----------|---------------------|------------------|
| Clinical Optometry | 35% | 35 questions | Critical |
| Ocular Disease & Pathology | 25% | 25 questions | Critical |
| Optical Dispensing & Refraction | 15% | 15 questions | High |
| Professional Ethics & Regulations | 15% | 15 questions | High |
| Pediatric & Special Populations | 10% | 10 questions | Moderate |

### Critical Understanding

The weightage system reflects the DHA's emphasis on clinical competency and patient safety. The combined 60% weightage for Clinical Optometry and Ocular Disease underscores the importance of diagnostic and clinical management skills. Candidates must achieve minimum competency thresholds in critical sections:

- **Clinical Optometry**: Minimum 50% score required
- **Ocular Disease & Pathology**: Minimum 50% score required
- **Professional Ethics**: Minimum 60% score required

## 2. Clinical Optometry (35% - 35 Questions)

### 2.1 Refraction and Optical Correction (12 questions - 34% of section)

This subcategory forms the backbone of optometric practice and receives significant examination emphasis.

#### Topic Breakdown:

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Subjective Refraction Techniques | 3 | Jackson Cross Cylinder, Fogging, Duochrome |
| Objective Refraction | 2 | Retinoscopy, Autorefraction |
| Astigmatism Correction | 2 | Axis determination, Power verification |
| Presbyopia Management | 2 | Near add determination, Bifocal/Progressive lenses |
| Anisometropia Management | 1 | Correction strategies, Adaptation issues |
| Refractive Surgery Co-management | 1 | Pre/post-operative care, IOL calculations |
| Special Refraction Cases | 1 | Pediatric, Low vision, Keratoconus |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Jackson Cross Cylinder (JCC) Technique**: Questions frequently test the correct positioning and interpretation of JCC for both axis and power determination. Remember: handle aligned with axis for axis determination; handle at 45° to axis for power determination.

2. **Retinoscopy Reflex Interpretation**: Candidates must understand "with" and "against" movement, speed of reflex, and scissors reflex indications. A common question format presents a reflex description and asks for the corresponding refractive status.

3. **Duochrome Test Interpretation**: The red-green test is frequently tested. Remember: "RAP" - Red clearer = Add Plus (or reduce minus). If the patient sees red clearer, they are slightly myopic from the endpoint.

4. **Presbyopia Add Calculation**: Questions often present a patient's age and working distance to calculate appropriate near addition. Know Hofstetter's formulas:
   - Minimum Amplitude = 15 - (0.25 × age)
   - Average Amplitude = 18.5 - (0.3 × age)
   - Maximum Amplitude = 25 - (0.4 × age)

#### Sample Question Concepts:

- Calculation of spherical equivalent from a given prescription
- Transposition between plus and minus cylinder forms
- Prism induced by decentered lenses
- Effective power changes with vertex distance

### 2.2 Binocular Vision Assessment (8 questions - 23% of section)

Binocular vision disorders are common in clinical practice and receive substantial examination focus.

#### Topic Breakdown:

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Cover Testing Techniques | 2 | Cover-uncover, Alternate cover, Prism cover |
| Heterophoria Measurement | 2 | Maddox rod, Von Graefe, Phoropter methods |
| Accommodative Disorders | 2 | Accommodative insufficiency, Excess, Infacility |
| Vergence Disorders | 1 | Convergence insufficiency, Divergence excess |
| Strabismus Classification | 1 | Comitant vs Incomitant, Paralytic vs Non-paralytic |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Cover Test Interpretation**: Understand the difference between phoria (latent) and tropia (manifest). Questions often describe eye movements during cover testing and ask for diagnosis.

2. **Convergence Insufficiency**: This is the most common binocular vision disorder. Know the diagnostic criteria:
   - Near exophoria > distance exophoria by 4+ prism diopters
   - Low positive fusional vergence
   - Reduced near point of convergence

3. **Accommodative Facility Testing**: Normal values are 10-12 cycles/minute binocularly and 11-14 cycles/minute monocularly. Reduced facility indicates accommodative dysfunction.

4. **AC/A Ratio**: Calculate using gradient method or heterophoria method. Normal AC/A ratio is 3:1 to 5:1.

### 2.3 Contact Lens Practice (8 questions - 23% of section)

Contact lens fitting and management is an essential optometric skill.

#### Topic Breakdown:

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Soft Lens Fitting | 2 | Base curve, Diameter, Movement assessment |
| Rigid Gas Permeable (RGP) Fitting | 2 | Fluorescein pattern interpretation |
| Toric Lens Fitting | 1 | Stabilization methods, Rotation compensation |
| Multifocal Contact Lenses | 1 | Simultaneous vs Alternating designs |
| Contact Lens Complications | 2 | Infections, Hypoxia, Mechanical issues |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Soft Lens Fitting Assessment**: Ideal movement is 0.5-1.0 mm on blink. Tight lens shows no movement; loose lens shows excessive movement and lens lag.

2. **RGP Fluorescein Patterns**: Essential to recognize:
   - Apical clearance (green pool at apex) = Steep fit
   - Apical touch (dark area at apex) = Flat fit
   - Ideal pattern shows mild apical clearance with mid-peripheral bearing

3. **Contact Lens Complications**: Know the stages of hypoxia:
   - Microcysts (early sign)
   - Neovascularization (advanced)
   - Corneal edema

### 2.4 Ocular Examination Techniques (7 questions - 20% of section)

#### Topic Breakdown:

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Visual Acuity Assessment | 2 | Snellen, LogMAR, Contrast sensitivity |
| Visual Field Testing | 2 | Perimetry, Field defects interpretation |
| Pupil Examination | 1 | RAPD, Light-near dissociation |
| Anterior Segment Examination | 1 | Slit lamp techniques, Van Herick |
| Fundus Examination | 1 | Direct/Indirect ophthalmoscopy |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **RAPD Detection**: Marcus Gunn pupil is detected by swinging flashlight test. The affected pupil dilates paradoxically when light swings to it.

2. **Van Herick Grading**: Essential for angle assessment:
   - Grade 4: AC depth = corneal thickness (open angle)
   - Grade 3: AC depth = 1/2 to 1/4 CT (open angle)
   - Grade 2: AC depth = 1/4 CT (possibly occludable)
   - Grade 1: AC depth < 1/4 CT (occludable angle)

## 3. Ocular Disease & Pathology (25% - 25 Questions)

### 3.1 Anterior Segment Diseases (10 questions - 40% of section)

#### Topic Breakdown:

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Conjunctival Disorders | 2 | Conjunctivitis types, Pinguecula, Pterygium |
| Corneal Disorders | 3 | Keratitis, Dystrophies, Abrasions/Ulcers |
| Eyelid Disorders | 2 | Blepharitis, Hordeolum, Chalazion |
| Dry Eye Disease | 2 | Diagnosis, Severity grading, Management |
| Scleral Disorders | 1 | Episcleritis, Scleritis |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Differential Diagnosis of Red Eye**: A classic examination topic. Differentiate between:
   - Conjunctivitis: Discharge, follicules/papillae, no pain
   - Iritis: Ciliary injection, cells/flare, pain
   - Keratitis: Corneal involvement, pain, photophobia
   - Acute glaucoma: Elevated IOP, halos, nausea

2. **Corneal Ulcer Types**: Know the distinguishing features:
   - Bacterial: Peripheral, localized, hypopyon
   - Fungal: Feathery edges, satellite lesions
   - Viral (HSV): Dendritic pattern
   - Acanthamoeba: Ring infiltrate, severe pain

3. **Dry Eye Classification**: Evaporative (MGD) vs Aqueous deficient (Sjögren's). Diagnostic tests include TBUT, Schirmer's, and osmolarity.

### 3.2 Glaucoma (6 questions - 24% of section)

Glaucoma is a critical topic due to its prevalence and potential for vision loss.

#### Topic Breakdown:

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Glaucoma Types | 2 | POAG, PACG, Secondary glaucomas |
| Diagnosis | 2 | IOP, Visual fields, Optic disc assessment |
| Medical Management | 1 | Topical medications, Mechanisms |
| Surgical Management | 1 | Laser procedures, Filtering surgery |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **ISNT Rule**: The neuroretinal rim should follow the pattern: Inferior ≥ Superior ≥ Nasal ≥ Temporal. Violation suggests glaucomatous damage.

2. **Cup-Disc Ratio**: Normal is 0.3-0.4. C/D > 0.6 or asymmetry > 0.2 is suspicious. Know that thin corneas underestimate IOP and are a risk factor.

3. **Visual Field Defects in Glaucoma**: Progressive pattern:
   - Early: Paracentral scotoma, Nasal step
   - Moderate: Arcuate defect, Partial ring
   - Advanced: Complete ring, Central/timestamp island

4. **IOP-Lowering Medications**: Know mechanisms and side effects:
   - Prostaglandin analogs: Increased uveoscleral outflow
   - Beta-blockers: Decreased aqueous production
   - Alpha-agonists: Both mechanisms
   - Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors: Decreased aqueous production

### 3.3 Retinal Diseases (5 questions - 20% of section)

#### Topic Breakdown:

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Diabetic Retinopathy | 2 | Staging, Management, Screening |
| Age-Related Macular Degeneration | 1 | Dry vs Wet, Treatment options |
| Retinal Vascular Diseases | 1 | CRAO, CRVO, BRVO |
| Retinal Detachment | 1 | Types, Warning signs, Management |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Diabetic Retinopathy Staging**:
   - Mild NPDR: Microaneurysms only
   - Moderate NPDR: Microaneurysms + hemorrhages + hard exudates
   - Severe NPDR: 4-2-1 rule (diffuse hemorrhages, venous beading, IRMA)
   - Proliferative DR: Neovascularization, vitreous hemorrhage

2. **AMD Features**:
   - Dry (atrophic): Drusen, Geographic atrophy, No treatment
   - Wet (exudative): CNV, Anti-VEGF therapy

3. **Retinal Detachment Warning Signs**: Flashing lights, Floaters, Curtain effect. High myopia and previous cataract surgery are risk factors.

### 3.4 Neuro-Ophthalmic Conditions (4 questions - 16% of section)

#### Topic Breakdown:

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Optic Nerve Disorders | 2 | Optic neuritis, Ischemic optic neuropathy |
| Cranial Nerve Palsies | 1 | CN III, IV, VI palsies |
| Visual Field Pathways | 1 | Lesion localization |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Visual Field Defect Localization**:
   - Pre-chiasmal: Unilateral defects
   - Chiasmal: Bitemporal hemianopia
   - Post-chiasmal: Homonymous defects

2. **Optic Neuritis**: Classic presentation includes:
   - Young female
   - Pain with eye movement
   - Central scotoma
   - RAPD present
   - MRI for MS evaluation

## 4. Optical Dispensing & Refraction (15% - 15 Questions)

### 4.1 Lens Design and Properties (6 questions)

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Lens Materials | 2 | Refractive index, Abbe value, Impact resistance |
| Lens Designs | 2 | Single vision, Bifocals, Progressives |
| Specialty Lenses | 1 | High-index, Aspheric, Atoric |
| Lens Treatments | 1 | AR coating, Photochromic, Tints |

### 4.2 Prism and Optics (5 questions)

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Prism Calculations | 2 | Prentice's rule, Compounding/Canceling |
| Optical Centers | 1 | Decentration, Induced prism |
| Aniseikonia | 1 | Image size differences, Knapp's law |
| Magnification | 1 | Spectacle vs Contact lens |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Prentice's Rule**: Prism = Lens power × Decentration (cm)
   Example: A +4.00D lens decentered 5mm induces 2 prism diopters

2. **Compounding and Canceling Prisms**:
   - Both bases same direction = Compounding (add)
   - Bases opposite directions = Canceling (subtract)

3. **Bifocal Segment Types and Applications**:
   - Flat-top: Most common, wide near field
   - Round: Less visible segment line
   - Executive: Full-width segment, good for high adds

### 5.2 Professional Conduct (5 questions)

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Patient Rights | 2 | Consent, Confidentiality, Records |
| Ethical Decision-Making | 2 | Case scenarios, Professional boundaries |
| Professional Misconduct | 1 | Definitions, Consequences |

### 5.3 Medical-Legal Issues (5 questions)

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Record Keeping | 2 | Documentation standards, Retention |
| Professional Liability | 2 | Malpractice, Insurance |
| Referral Guidelines | 1 | When to refer, Documentation |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Scope of Practice Limitations**: DHA optometrists must NOT:
   - Perform surgical procedures
   - Inject pharmaceutical agents
   - Prescribe oral medications
   - Diagnose conditions requiring ophthalmologist referral

2. **Mandatory Referral Conditions**:
   - Glaucoma suspect or confirmed
   - Cataract affecting vision
   - Retinal pathology
   - Diabetic retinopathy (proliferative)
   - Neuro-ophthalmic conditions

3. **CE Requirements**: 25 hours annually including:
   - Clinical Optometry: 10 hours minimum
   - Ocular Disease: 8 hours minimum
   - Professional Ethics: 4 hours minimum
   - Practice Management: 3 hours minimum

## 6. Pediatric & Special Populations (10% - 10 Questions)

### 6.1 Pediatric Optometry (5 questions)

| Topic | Questions | Key Concepts |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Pediatric Examination | 2 | Age-appropriate techniques, VAEstimation |
| Amblyopia | 2 | Types, Treatment, Critical period |
| Strabismus in Children | 1 | Esotropia, Exotropia, Management |

#### High-Yield Topics:

1. **Amblyopia Types and Treatment**:
   - Strabismic: Treat strabismus first, then patching
   - Anisometropic: Correct refractive error, patching
   - Deprivation: Urgent treatment (cataract, ptosis)
   - Critical period: Before age 7-8 years

2. **Pediatric Vision Screening**: Know age-appropriate tests:
   - 0-6 months: Fix and follow, OKN, VEP
   - 6-24 months: Fix and follow, Cover test, Teller cards
   - 2-5 years: Picture charts, HOTV, Lea symbols
   - 5+ years: Snellen, LogMAR

## 7. High-Yield Areas Summary

### Critical Topics for Maximum Score Impact

Based on weightage and frequency of testing, prioritize the following areas:

#### Tier 1 - Highest Priority (Focus 40% of study time)

1. **Refraction Techniques**: JCC, Retinoscopy, Duochrome (12 questions)
2. **Ocular Disease Diagnosis**: Red eye differential, Corneal conditions (10 questions)
3. **Glaucoma**: Diagnosis, management, visual fields (6 questions)
4. **Binocular Vision**: Cover testing, Accommodative disorders (8 questions)

#### Tier 2 - High Priority (Focus 30% of study time)

5. **Contact Lens Fitting**: Soft, RGP, Complications (8 questions)
6. **Optical Principles**: Prism, Lens design (11 questions)
7. **DHA Regulations**: Scope, Ethics, Licensing (15 questions)

#### Tier 3 - Important (Focus 20% of study time)

8. **Retinal Diseases**: DR, AMD, RVO (5 questions)
9. **Neuro-Ophthalmology**: Field defects, Optic nerve (4 questions)
10. **Pediatric Optometry**: Amblyopia, Strabismus (5 questions)

#### Tier 4 - Standard (Focus 10% of study time)

11. **Special Populations**: Geriatric, Low vision (5 questions)
12. **Emerging Topics**: Research, Technology (variable)

| 11 | Special Populations (10%) | Pediatric, Geriatric |
| 12 | Review & Practice | Mock exams, High-yield review |

### Recommended Resources

1. **Kanski's Clinical Ophthalmology**: Comprehensive disease reference
2. **Borish's Clinical Refraction**: Refraction techniques
3. **DHA Health Regulation Guidelines**: Official regulations
4. **AAO Preferred Practice Patterns**: Clinical guidelines
5. **Wills Eye Manual**: Emergency management

### Examination Tips

1. **Read questions carefully**: Watch for "NOT," "EXCEPT," "MOST LIKELY"
2. **Time management**: 1.5 minutes per question average
3. **Eliminate wrong answers**: Process of elimination improves odds
4. **Flag difficult questions**: Return after completing easier ones
5. **Clinical reasoning**: Apply practical knowledge, not just memorization

## Conclusion

Success in the DHA Optometry Examination requires a strategic approach that aligns study efforts with the official syllabus weightage. Clinical Optometry and Ocular Disease together comprise 60% of the examination and should receive proportionate attention. Understanding topic percentages allows candidates to allocate study time efficiently and prioritize high-yield areas.

Remember that the examination tests not only knowledge recall but also clinical reasoning and professional judgment. Regular practice with case-based questions and familiarity with DHA-specific regulations will enhance examination performance.
