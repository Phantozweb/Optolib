## Practice Questions

### Clinical Optometry

**Question 1:**
A 45-year-old patient presents with blurred vision at distance. Retinoscopy findings reveal +2.00 DS in both eyes with no astigmatic component. The most likely diagnosis is:

A. Myopia
B. Hyperopia
C. Astigmatism
D. Presbyopia
E. Emmetropia

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The retinoscopy findings show +2.00 DS in both eyes, indicating hyperopia. In hyperopia, light rays focus behind the retina when accommodation is relaxed.

---

**Question 2:**
During subjective refraction, the Jackson cross cylinder test reveals that the patient sees better when the minus cylinder axis is at 90°. The correct action is to:

A. Add +0.25 DC at 90°
B. Add -0.25 DC at 90°
C. Add +0.25 DC at 180°
D. Add -0.25 DC at 180°
E. Keep the prescription unchanged

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** When the patient sees better with minus cylinder at 90°, it indicates the need for more minus cylinder power at that axis.

---

**Question 3:**
A patient's visual acuity is 6/12 in the right eye and 6/6 in the left eye. The pinhole test improves the right eye to 6/6. This indicates:

A. Amblyopia
B. Refractive error
C. Retinal pathology
D. Optic nerve disease
E. Cataract

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Improvement with pinhole indicates a refractive error. The pinhole reduces blur by limiting light rays to the central optical axis.

---

### Ocular Disease

**Question 4:**
A 60-year-old diabetic patient presents with sudden painless vision loss. Fundus examination shows flame-shaped hemorrhages in all quadrants, venous dilation, and cotton wool spots. The most likely diagnosis is:

A. Non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy
B. Proliferative diabetic retinopathy
C. Central retinal vein occlusion
D. Central retinal artery occlusion
E. Hypertensive retinopathy

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The combination of flame-shaped hemorrhages in all quadrants, venous dilation, and cotton wool spots is characteristic of central retinal vein occlusion (CRVO).

---

**Question 5:**
In primary open-angle glaucoma, which of the following is most characteristic?

A. Elevated IOP with open angle
B. Normal IOP with closed angle
C. Elevated IOP with closed angle
D. Normal IOP with optic nerve damage
E. Fluctuating IOP with open angle

**Correct Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Primary open-angle glaucoma is characterized by elevated intraocular pressure with an open anterior chamber angle.

---

**Question 6:**
A 35-year-old patient presents with a red, painful eye, photophobia, and small irregular pupil. Slit lamp examination reveals cells and flare in the anterior chamber. The most appropriate initial treatment is:

A. Topical antibiotics
B. Topical corticosteroids
C. Topical beta-blockers
D. Oral acetazolamide
E. Topical NSAIDs

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The clinical picture suggests anterior uveitis (iritis). The treatment of choice is topical corticosteroids to reduce inflammation.

---

### Pharmacology

**Question 7:**
Which of the following glaucoma medications works by decreasing aqueous humor production?

A. Latanoprost
B. Brimonidine
C. Timolol
D. Pilocarpine
E. Acetazolamide (all of the above except pilocarpine)

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Timolol is a beta-blocker that reduces aqueous humor production by the ciliary body.

---

**Question 8:**
The recommended duration for cycloplegic refraction in children is:

A. 30 minutes after instillation
B. 45 minutes after instillation  
C. 60 minutes after instillation
D. 30-60 minutes depending on the agent
E. 90 minutes after instillation

**Correct Answer: D**

**Explanation:** The timing depends on the cycloplegic agent used. Tropicamide peaks at 20-40 minutes, cyclopentolate at 30-60 minutes.

---

### Contact Lenses

**Question 9:**
A patient wearing soft contact lenses presents with a red eye and a small white lesion on the cornea with surrounding edema. The most likely diagnosis is:

A. Corneal abrasion
B. Contact lens-related corneal ulcer
C. Giant papillary conjunctivitis
D. Superior limbic keratoconjunctivitis
E. Corneal neovascularization

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:** A white lesion with surrounding edema in a contact lens wearer suggests a corneal ulcer, likely bacterial.

---

**Question 10:**
When fitting a soft contact lens, if the lens shows excessive movement with the blink test, the appropriate adjustment is to:

A. Decrease base curve (steeper)
B. Increase base curve (flatter)
C. Increase diameter
D. Decrease diameter
E. Change lens material

**Correct Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Excessive movement indicates a loose fit. Decreasing the base curve (making it steeper/tighter) will improve the fit.
