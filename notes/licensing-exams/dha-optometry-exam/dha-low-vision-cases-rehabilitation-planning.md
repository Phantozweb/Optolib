# DHA Low Vision Cases - Rehabilitation Planning

## Introduction

Clinical case-based questions are an essential component of the DHA Dubai optometry licensing examination. This section provides comprehensive case studies.

## Case 1: Diabetic Retinopathy

### Patient Presentation
- **Age:** 52 years
- **History:** Type 2 diabetes mellitus for 15 years, hypertension
- **Presenting complaint:** Gradual blurring of vision over 6 months
- **Last HbA1c:** 8.2%

### Clinical Findings
- Visual Acuity: RE 6/12, LE 6/18
- IOP: RE 16 mmHg, LE 18 mmHg

### Fundus Examination
| Finding | Right Eye | Left Eye |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Microaneurysms | Present | Present |
| Hemorrhages | Moderate | Moderate |
| Hard exudates | Few | Present in macula |
| Cotton wool spots | Absent | 1 present |

### Questions and Answers

**Q1: What is the staging?**
A: Right Eye - Moderate NPDR; Left Eye - Severe NPDR

**Q2: Additional tests needed?**
A: OCT for macular edema, Fluorescein angiography, HbA1c testing

**Q3: Management?**
A: Ophthalmology referral 2-4 weeks, Optimize glycemic control

## Case 2: Acute Angle-Closure Glaucoma

### Patient Presentation
- **Age:** 65 years, Female
- **Complaint:** Sudden severe right eye pain with headache
- **Duration:** 6 hours
- **Past history:** Hyperopia

### Clinical Findings
- Visual Acuity: RE 6/60, LE 6/6
- IOP: RE 52 mmHg, LE 18 mmHg
- Pupils: Right mid-dilated, fixed

### Questions and Answers

**Q1: Diagnosis?**
A: Acute angle-closure glaucoma (AACG)

**Q2: Immediate management?**
A: IV acetazolamide, Topical pilocarpine, Topical beta-blocker, Urgent ophthalmology referral

**Q3: Definitive treatment?**
A: Laser peripheral iridotomy (both eyes)

## Case 3: Pediatric Vision

### Patient Presentation
- **Age:** 4 years, Male
- **History:** Parent reports squinting at TV
- **Family history:** Father has myopia

### Clinical Findings
- Visual Acuity: RE 6/12, LE 6/30
- Cover test: Left exotropia (15 PD)
- Cycloplegic refraction: RE +1.00 DS, LE +3.50 DS

### Questions and Answers

**Q1: Diagnosis?**
A: Anisometropic amblyopia (left eye), Sensory exotropia

**Q2: Management?**
A: Prescribe glasses, Occlusion therapy, Monitor every 3 months

## Case 4: Contact Lens Complication

### Patient Presentation
- **Age:** 28 years, Female
- **History:** Extended wear soft lenses, sleeps with lenses
- **Complaint:** Right eye redness and pain for 2 days

### Clinical Findings
- Visual Acuity: RE 6/18
- Slit lamp: 2mm white infiltrate with epithelial defect

### Questions and Answers

**Q1: Diagnosis?**
A: Contact lens-related corneal ulcer (microbial keratitis)

**Q2: Management?**
A: Discontinue lenses, Corneal scraping, Broad-spectrum antibiotics, Urgent ophthalmology referral

---

## Key Clinical Pearls

1. **Always measure IOP** in red eye presentations
2. **Document visual acuity** before any examination
3. **Consider systemic associations** in ocular conditions
4. **Know your referral criteria** and urgency levels
