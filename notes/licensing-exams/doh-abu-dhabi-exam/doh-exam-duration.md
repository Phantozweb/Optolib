# DOH Exam Duration & Sections

## Examination Structure Overview

The Department of Health (DOH) Abu Dhabi optometry licensing examination is designed to comprehensively assess candidates' readiness for independent practice. The examination structure reflects international standards while incorporating UAE-specific clinical guidelines.

## Total Examination Duration

| Component | Duration | Format |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Written Examination | 3 hours | 100 MCQs |
| Clinical Assessment | 2 hours | 4-6 stations (if applicable) |
| Total Testing Time | 5 hours | Including breaks |

## Written Examination Breakdown

### Section 1: Basic Sciences (30 minutes, 25 questions)

**Topics Covered:**
- Ocular anatomy and embryology
- Visual physiology
- Ocular biochemistry
- Microbiology relevant to eye
- Basic pharmacology principles

**Question Characteristics:**
- Foundational knowledge assessment
- Direct recall questions
- Diagram interpretation
- Short clinical scenarios

### Section 2: Clinical Optometry (60 minutes, 35 questions)

**Topics Covered:**
- Refraction techniques (subjective and objective)
- Binocular vision assessment
- Visual field testing
- Ocular health examination
- Special testing procedures

**Question Characteristics:**
- Clinical scenario-based
- Procedure sequencing
- Instrument interpretation
- Patient management decisions

### Section 3: Ocular Disease (60 minutes, 25 questions)

**Topics Covered:**
- Anterior segment diseases
- Posterior segment pathology
- Glaucoma diagnosis and management
- Ocular emergencies
- Systemic disease manifestations

**Question Characteristics:**
- Case-based diagnosis
- Management decisions
- Referral criteria
- Treatment selection

### Section 4: Professional Practice (30 minutes, 15 questions)

**Topics Covered:**
- UAE healthcare regulations
- Professional ethics
- Documentation standards
- Public health optometry
- Practice management

**Question Characteristics:**
- Legal and ethical scenarios
- Cultural considerations
- Quality assurance
- Interprofessional relations

The Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) component may include:
- Standardized patient interactions
- Demonstration of clinical skills
- Case presentation to examiner
- Emergency scenario management

## Time Management Strategies

### Per-Question Time Allocation

**Written Examination:**
- Average: 1.8 minutes per question
- Strategy: First pass in 1.5 minutes, flag difficult questions
- Review: Reserve 15-20 minutes for flagged questions

### Recommended Approach

1. **First Pass (90 minutes):**
   - Answer straightforward questions immediately
   - Flag complex scenarios for review
   - Aim for 60-70% completion

2. **Second Pass (45 minutes):**
   - Address flagged questions
   - Apply clinical reasoning
   - Eliminate obviously wrong options

3. **Final Review (15 minutes):**
   - Check for skipped questions
   - Verify answer selections
   - Trust first instincts on uncertain answers

## Key Considerations

### What to Bring
- Valid photo identification (passport/Emirates ID)
- DOH examination confirmation letter
- Prescription glasses (if needed)
- Light snacks for breaks

### What NOT to Bring
- Electronic devices
- Study materials
- Bags (locker provided)
- Food in testing room

### Testing Environment
- Climate-controlled room
- Individual testing stations
- Noise-reducing headphones available
- Proctor assistance available

## Scoring and Results

### Score Calculation
- Correct answers counted
- No negative marking
- Percentage calculated from total correct
- Section scores provided for feedback

### Pass Threshold
- Minimum passing score: 60-65%
- May vary by examination cycle
- Adjusted for difficulty level

### Result Timeline
- Provisional results: Immediately post-exam
- Official results: 2-4 weeks
- Score report: Detailed breakdown provided

## Adaptations for Special Circumstances

### Extended Time
- Available for documented disabilities
- Arranged through prior application
- Additional 25-50% time typically granted

### Other Accommodations
- Screen readers for visual impairments
- Sign language interpreters
- Adjustable workstation height
- Frequent breaks for medical conditions
